Income Tax Quick Summary

Malaysia individual income tax is generally based on annual income, allowable tax reliefs, chargeable income and resident tax rates.

Annual Income tax is assessed by year of assessment
Progressive Higher chargeable income may fall into higher tax brackets
Reliefs Tax reliefs reduce chargeable income
PCB Monthly tax deductions may offset final tax payable

What Is Income Tax in Malaysia?

Income tax is tax charged on taxable income earned by individuals. For employees, common income sources include salary, bonus, commission, allowances and benefits that may be taxable under Malaysia tax rules.

Your final tax payable is not based only on monthly salary. It usually depends on total annual income, deductions, tax relief claims, rebates and PCB already deducted by your employer.

How Malaysia Income Tax Works

Step 1

Calculate Annual Income

Add salary, bonus, commission and other taxable employment income for the year.

Step 2

Claim Tax Reliefs

Deduct eligible personal reliefs such as individual relief, EPF, insurance, medical, education and lifestyle claims.

Step 3

Apply Tax Rates

Resident individual tax is generally charged using progressive tax brackets.

Step 4

Offset PCB / Rebates

Monthly PCB deductions and eligible rebates may reduce the balance tax payable.

Malaysia Resident Income Tax Rates

Malaysia uses a progressive income tax system. This means higher chargeable income may be taxed at higher rates.

Chargeable Income Tax Rate
First RM5,000 0%
Next RM15,000 1% – 3%
Higher Income Bands Progressively higher rates
Note: Tax rates may change from time to time. Always refer to the latest LHDN guidance for current resident tax brackets.

Resident vs Non-Resident Taxpayer

Tax residency status can significantly affect how your income is taxed in Malaysia. Individuals who qualify as Malaysian tax residents are generally eligible for personal tax reliefs, tax rebates and progressive resident tax rates.

In contrast, non-resident individuals may not be eligible for most personal reliefs and may be subject to different tax treatment under Malaysia income tax rules.

Category General Treatment
Resident Usually eligible for tax reliefs and progressive tax rates.
Non-Resident Generally not eligible for most personal tax reliefs.

Residency status generally depends on your circumstances and the number of days present in Malaysia during the relevant year.

What Income Is Usually Taxable?

Employment income is one of the most common forms of taxable income in Malaysia. Besides basic salary, employees may also receive bonuses, commissions, incentives and allowances that could form part of taxable income.

Individuals with multiple income sources should ensure all taxable income is properly declared during tax filing.

  • Employment salary and wages
  • Bonus and commission
  • Allowances and benefits-in-kind
  • Business income
  • Rental income
  • Freelance and consulting income

What Income May Not Be Taxable?

Certain receipts may not be taxable depending on the circumstances and applicable tax rules. However, tax treatment depends on the facts, source of income and latest LHDN guidance.

  • Certain gifts received personally
  • Selected tax-exempt income categories
  • Specific exemptions announced by the government
  • Certain reimbursements that are properly supported
Practical note: If you are not sure whether an item is taxable, keep the supporting documents and check the latest LHDN guidance or get professional advice.

Simple Income Tax Formula

Tax Payable = Tax on Chargeable Income − Rebates − PCB Paid Chargeable income is generally income after allowable deductions and reliefs.

Common Income Tax Items to Check

Before filing your income tax, prepare the main documents and records that affect your annual income, tax reliefs and PCB deductions.

  • EA Form income and PCB amount from employer
  • EPF, SOCSO and insurance contribution records
  • Tax relief receipts and supporting documents
  • Other income such as rental, business, commission or freelance income
  • Whether you are filing as resident or non-resident
  • Previous year tax refund, balance payable or instalment records if any

Income Tax vs PCB

Income tax and PCB are related, but they are not exactly the same. PCB is a monthly tax deduction made through payroll, while income tax is the final annual tax calculation after considering total income, tax reliefs, rebates and PCB already paid.

Item Meaning When It Happens
Income Tax Final tax assessed for the year During tax filing
PCB Monthly tax deduction from salary Every month through payroll
Tax Relief Eligible claim that reduces chargeable income Claimed during tax filing or TP1 update

Malaysia Income Tax Filing Process

Step 1

Collect Documents

Prepare your EA Form, income records and supporting receipts.

Step 2

Calculate Reliefs

Review eligible tax relief claims and supporting documents.

Step 3

Submit e-Filing

Complete your tax return through the LHDN e-Filing system.

Step 4

Refund or Balance Tax

Receive a refund or pay any outstanding tax after assessment.

For most employees, the income tax filing process begins with obtaining the EA Form from their employer. The EA Form contains important information such as annual employment income and PCB deductions made during the year.

Taxpayers should also gather receipts and supporting documents for any tax reliefs they intend to claim. Supporting documents are generally not submitted together with the return, but should be retained for future reference in case verification is required.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is income tax calculated monthly or yearly in Malaysia?

Income tax is generally assessed yearly. However, employees may pay tax monthly through PCB deductions handled by the employer.

Is PCB the same as final income tax?

No. PCB is a monthly deduction. Final income tax is calculated when you file your tax return, after considering total income, reliefs, rebates and PCB already paid.

Can tax relief reduce income tax?

Yes. Eligible tax reliefs reduce chargeable income, which may lower the final tax payable.

Do I need to file tax if PCB was already deducted?

PCB deduction does not automatically replace tax filing. If you are required to file, you should submit the relevant tax return and declare income and reliefs correctly.

Why is my tax calculator result different from LHDN?

Differences may come from relief claims, rebates, resident status, bonus treatment, PCB already paid or updated LHDN rules.

What documents should I keep for income tax?

You should keep EA Forms, income records, receipts for tax relief claims, insurance statements, EPF records and other supporting documents related to your tax return.

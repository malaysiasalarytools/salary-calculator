What Is a Loan?

A loan is money borrowed from a bank, financial institution or lender that must be repaid over time, usually with interest. In Malaysia, common loans include home loans, personal loans, car loans and business loans.

Before taking a loan, it is important to understand the monthly repayment, interest cost, loan tenure and how the new commitment affects your monthly cash flow.

Common Types of Loans in Malaysia

🏠

Home Loan

Used to finance a property purchase, usually with a long repayment tenure.

💳

Personal Loan

Unsecured financing for personal needs, usually with fixed monthly instalments.

🚗

Car Loan

Used to finance a vehicle purchase over a fixed hire purchase period.

🏢

Business Loan

Financing used for business working capital, assets or expansion.

How Loan Repayment Works

Loan repayment usually depends on the loan amount, interest rate and repayment period. A longer tenure may reduce monthly instalment, but it can increase total interest paid over time.

Monthly Commitment = Loan Instalment + Existing Debt PaymentsUse this amount when checking affordability and DSR.

Example Loan Affordability Check

If your monthly net income is RM4,000 and your total monthly debt commitments are RM1,200, your debt commitment uses 30% of your income.

  • Monthly income: RM4,000
  • Total monthly debt: RM1,200
  • Estimated DSR: 30%

This does not guarantee approval, but it gives a simple starting point to understand whether a new loan may be affordable.

Things to Check Before Taking a Loan

Monthly Instalment

Make sure the repayment fits your salary and monthly expenses.

Interest Cost

Compare the total cost, not only the monthly amount.

Loan Tenure

Longer tenure may look cheaper monthly but can cost more overall.

Emergency Buffer

Keep cash reserves before adding long-term debt commitments.

Planning Note: A loan should improve your financial position or solve a real need. Avoid borrowing only because the monthly repayment looks affordable.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I check before applying for a loan?

Check your monthly instalment, total interest cost, loan tenure, existing commitments and emergency savings before applying.

Does salary affect loan approval?

Yes. Salary is one of the key factors lenders use to assess repayment ability, together with credit profile, employment stability and existing debts.

What is DSR in loan applications?

DSR stands for Debt Service Ratio. It compares your monthly debt commitments against your monthly income.

Is a lower monthly instalment always better?

Not always. A lower instalment may come from a longer tenure, which can increase the total interest paid.

Can I take a loan if I already have other debts?

It depends on your income, DSR, repayment record and lender requirements. Existing debts reduce your available borrowing capacity.

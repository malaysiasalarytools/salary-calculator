Annual leave is paid time off given to employees each year. In Malaysia, the minimum annual leave entitlement depends mainly on the employee's length of service.

8 Days Less than 2 years of service
12 Days 2 to 5 years of service
16 Days More than 5 years of service
Paid Leave Annual leave is normally paid time off

What Is Annual Leave?

Annual leave is paid leave given to employees so they can take time off work while still receiving salary. It is one of the key employment benefits employees look at when comparing salary packages and job offers.

In Malaysia, annual leave entitlement is commonly based on the employee's length of service. Employers may provide more leave than the statutory minimum, but they should not provide less than the minimum entitlement required for eligible employees.

Annual Leave Entitlement in Malaysia

Annual leave entitlement is generally linked to an employee's completed years of service. Employees with longer service periods are typically entitled to more annual leave days under the minimum employment standards.

The table below shows a simplified guide to minimum annual leave entitlement based on completed years of service.

Length of Service Minimum Annual Leave Simple Explanation
Less than 2 years 8 days per year For employees with shorter service period
2 years or more but less than 5 years 12 days per year For employees with mid-level service period
5 years or more 16 days per year For long-serving employees
Note: This is a simplified guide. Actual entitlement may depend on employment contract terms, company policy, eligibility and the latest employment law requirements.

How Annual Leave Is Calculated

Annual leave is usually calculated based on the employee's yearly entitlement. If an employee joins or leaves during the year, annual leave may be calculated on a pro-rated basis depending on company policy and applicable employment rules.

Pro-Rated Leave = Annual Entitlement × Months Worked ÷ 12 Example: 8 days × 6 months ÷ 12 = 4 days of annual leave

Example Annual Leave Calculation

If an employee is entitled to 8 days of annual leave per year and works for 6 months in the year, the estimated pro-rated leave may be:

  • Annual entitlement: 8 days
  • Months worked: 6 months
  • Estimated pro-rated leave: 4 days

Some companies may have specific rounding rules, cut-off dates or HR policies, so the actual result may differ slightly.

Annual Leave During Probation

Employees often ask whether they can take annual leave during probation. This depends on the employment contract and company policy. Some employers allow leave to be taken after confirmation, while others allow pro-rated leave during probation.

Even if annual leave is not normally taken during probation, employees may still accumulate entitlement depending on the employment arrangement and applicable rules.

Can Annual Leave Be Carried Forward?

Many companies allow employees to carry forward unused annual leave to the following year. However, carry-forward rules vary between employers.

Some companies allow leave to be carried forward for a limited period, while others require employees to use their leave before a specific deadline.

Employees should review company policies carefully to avoid losing unused leave entitlement.

Annual Leave During Resignation

Employees who resign often ask what happens to unused annual leave during the notice period. Depending on company policy and employment terms, unused leave may be taken during the notice period, paid out in cash or adjusted against final salary.

Employers may also have internal policies regarding whether annual leave can be taken during the notice period. Employees should check their employment contract and discuss arrangements with HR before submitting leave applications.

Common Annual Leave Mistakes

Many disputes between employees and employers arise because annual leave policies are misunderstood. Employees should always check company policies, approval requirements and carry-forward rules before making leave plans.

  • Assuming annual leave can be taken without approval
  • Not checking carry-forward deadlines
  • Misunderstanding pro-rated leave calculations
  • Using all leave before resignation without HR confirmation
  • Confusing annual leave with medical leave entitlement

Unused Annual Leave

Unused annual leave is a common issue during year-end, resignation or payroll processing. Depending on company policy, unused leave may be carried forward, forfeited or paid out in certain situations.

Employees should check their employment contract or HR policy to understand whether unused annual leave can be carried forward and whether there is any deadline to use it.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many days of annual leave are employees entitled to in Malaysia?

Minimum annual leave entitlement commonly depends on length of service: 8 days for less than 2 years, 12 days for 2 to 5 years and 16 days for more than 5 years of service.

Is annual leave paid in Malaysia?

Yes. Annual leave is normally paid time off, which means employees continue to receive salary while taking approved annual leave.

Can annual leave be pro-rated?

Yes. Annual leave is commonly pro-rated when an employee joins or leaves during the year. The calculation may depend on months worked, company policy and employment terms.

Can employees take annual leave during probation?

This depends on the employment contract and company policy. Some employers allow pro-rated annual leave during probation, while others require employees to wait until confirmation.

Can annual leave be taken during the notice period?

This depends on company policy, employment contract terms and employer approval. Some employers allow employees to use annual leave during the notice period, while others may restrict leave to ensure a proper handover of duties before the employee leaves the company.

Can annual leave be encashed?

Whether annual leave can be converted into cash depends on company policy and employment arrangements. In certain situations, unused annual leave may be paid out when an employee resigns or leaves the company, while other employers may require leave to be taken before the end of employment.

What happens to unused annual leave when resigning?

Unused annual leave may be paid out, offset against notice period or adjusted in final salary depending on company policy and employment terms.

Can employers reject annual leave applications?

Annual leave usually requires employer approval. Employers may reject or reschedule leave requests due to operational needs, staffing issues or company policy.

Can annual leave be carried forward to the next year?

Carry-forward rules depend on company policy or employment terms. Some companies allow unused leave to be carried forward for a limited period, while others may require leave to be used within the year.

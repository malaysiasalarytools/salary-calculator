How Income Tax Is Calculated in Malaysia

Income tax is tax charged on taxable income. For resident individuals in Malaysia, income tax is generally calculated using progressive tax rates. This means higher chargeable income may be taxed at higher tax brackets.

Chargeable Income = Annual Income − Tax Reliefs Estimated tax is then calculated using progressive resident individual tax brackets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this income tax calculator exact?

No. This calculator provides an estimate only. Actual tax may vary depending on reliefs, rebates, deductions, zakat and official LHDN calculation.

What is chargeable income?

Chargeable income is the estimated taxable amount after deducting allowable reliefs and deductions from annual income.

Is PCB the same as income tax?

No. PCB is monthly tax deduction through payroll. Final income tax is determined when annual tax return is submitted.

Can I use this for tax filing?

This tool is for estimation only. For official filing, refer to LHDN guidance, MyTax or a qualified tax professional.

Can tax relief reduce my income tax?

Yes. For resident taxpayers, eligible tax reliefs may reduce chargeable income and lower final tax payable.

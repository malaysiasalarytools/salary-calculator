What Is Maternity Leave?

Maternity leave is leave granted to eligible female employees for childbirth, recovery and early childcare. It allows employees to take time away from work while maintaining employment continuity and income protection during an important life event.

In Malaysia, maternity leave is important for both employees and employers because it affects salary, payroll, leave records, workforce planning and employment protection. Employees should understand how many days they may be entitled to, whether the leave is paid, what documents may be required and how to communicate with HR before the leave starts.

Maternity Leave Entitlement in Malaysia

A common statutory maternity leave reference in Malaysia is 98 days. This period is intended to give eligible female employees time for childbirth, recovery and childcare while preserving employment continuity during the approved leave period.

Some employers may provide maternity leave benefits that are better than the statutory minimum. This may depend on the employment contract, employee handbook, company HR policy, collective agreement or internal benefit structure.

Note: This guide is a simplified employment guide for general understanding. For official, legal or dispute situations, employees and employers should check the latest law, employment contract, company policy and professional advice.

Who Is Eligible for Maternity Leave?

Eligibility for maternity leave may depend on employment status, pregnancy, supporting documents, length of service, employment records and applicable employment requirements. Employees should confirm their specific entitlement with HR before the expected delivery date.

HR teams should review the employee’s employment contract, employee handbook and applicable rules before confirming maternity leave entitlement. Clear eligibility checks help avoid disputes about paid leave, payroll treatment and return-to-work arrangements.

Notice Requirements for Maternity Leave

Employees should notify their employer as early as possible before the expected delivery date. Early communication helps the employer plan workload coverage, temporary replacement arrangements, payroll processing and leave records.

Employers may require supporting documents according to company procedures and HR policies. Employees should keep written communication with HR, including expected leave dates, supporting documents, approval confirmation and any approved changes to the leave period.

Maternity Leave Pay

Eligible employees may receive maternity leave pay during the approved leave period, subject to applicable employment requirements and payroll procedures. Salary may be processed in the usual payroll cycle or according to company policy.

Employees should review payslips and salary records during maternity leave to understand how salary, deductions and contributions are reflected. If the paid amount is different from expectation, it is better to clarify with HR early instead of waiting until after the leave period ends.

EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB During Maternity Leave

Many employees ask whether EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB continue during maternity leave. The actual treatment may depend on whether salary is paid and how payroll is processed during the leave period.

If salary continues to be paid during maternity leave, statutory deductions or contributions may still appear on the payslip depending on payroll treatment. Employees should check their payslip, EPF record, SOCSO/EIS record and PCB deduction if any item looks unusual.

Payroll Item What Employees Should Check
EPF / KWSP Check whether employee and employer contributions are reflected correctly when salary is paid.
SOCSO / PERKESO Review payslip and contribution records if monthly salary is processed during maternity leave.
EIS Check whether EIS is processed according to payroll treatment for the month.
PCB Monthly tax deduction may depend on taxable employment income and payroll calculation for the month.
Net Salary Take-home pay may differ if salary, deductions or allowances are treated differently during leave.

Maternity Leave vs Annual Leave vs MC Leave

Maternity leave is different from annual leave and medical leave. Each leave type has a different purpose and should be recorded correctly in HR and payroll records.

Leave Type Purpose Common Payroll Meaning
Maternity Leave Childbirth, recovery and early childcare Paid if eligible, subject to employment requirements and payroll rules.
Annual Leave Planned paid time off Deducted from annual leave balance when approved.
MC Leave Illness or medical reasons supported by MC Recorded as sick leave or hospitalisation leave if eligible.

Returning to Work After Maternity Leave

Employees should communicate with HR and supervisors before returning to work. This helps ensure payroll records, work schedules, system access and administrative matters are updated correctly.

Planning ahead can make the transition back to work smoother for both employees and employers. Employees may also want to confirm their reporting date, work arrangement, remaining leave balance and any outstanding payroll matters before returning.

Common Maternity Leave Mistakes

Maternity leave disputes often happen because communication, documents or payroll expectations are not clear. Employees and employers should keep proper records and confirm arrangements in writing.

  • Not notifying the employer early enough
  • Missing supporting documents or expected delivery information
  • Not understanding eligibility requirements
  • Assuming all payroll items are treated the same during leave
  • Not checking payslip, salary and contribution records
  • Failing to confirm return-to-work arrangements

Frequently Asked Questions

How many days of maternity leave are available in Malaysia?

A common statutory maternity leave reference in Malaysia is 98 days. Employees should still check the latest employment requirements, contract terms and company policy for their specific situation.

Who qualifies for maternity leave?

Eligibility may depend on employment status, pregnancy, supporting documents and applicable employment requirements. Employees should confirm their entitlement with HR or refer to their employment contract and company policy.

Is maternity leave paid?

Eligible employees may receive paid maternity leave during the approved leave period, subject to applicable requirements and payroll procedures.

Can maternity leave be extended?

Any extension may depend on medical advice, company policy, employment terms and employer approval. If additional leave is needed, employees should inform HR as early as possible.

Do EPF, SOCSO and EIS continue during maternity leave?

The treatment may depend on whether salary is paid and how payroll is processed during the leave period. Employees should review payslips and contribution records.

Can an employer terminate an employee during maternity leave?

Maternity-related employment decisions should be handled carefully and consistently with employment rules. Employees who face disputes should keep written records and seek proper advice.

What documents are usually required for maternity leave?

Employers may request supporting documents such as expected delivery date information, medical documents or company leave forms, depending on internal HR procedures.

When should employees notify employers about maternity leave?

Employees should notify their employer as early as possible so the company can plan workload, payroll and temporary work arrangements.

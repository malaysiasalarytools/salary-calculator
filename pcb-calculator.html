What is PCB Tax in Malaysia?

PCB stands for Potongan Cukai Bulanan, also known as Monthly Tax Deduction. It is Malaysia's monthly income tax deduction system.

Instead of paying income tax in one large amount at the end of the year, PCB allows tax to be deducted gradually from monthly salary.

How Does PCB Work?

Employers calculate PCB based on employee income and deduct the amount from salary every month. The deducted PCB amount is then submitted to LHDN.

PCB helps employees spread their income tax payments across the year instead of paying everything at once.

Who Needs To Pay PCB?

Employees whose income exceeds the taxable threshold may be subject to PCB deductions.

Actual PCB depends on income level, tax reliefs, personal circumstances and current tax rules.

How Is PCB Calculated?

PCB is generally calculated based on estimated annual income. Higher income usually results in higher monthly PCB deductions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PCB mandatory in Malaysia?

Employers are generally required to deduct PCB from eligible employees according to applicable tax rules.

Is PCB the same as final income tax?

No. Final income tax is determined when the annual tax return is submitted.

Can PCB be refunded?

Yes. If excess tax has been deducted, the taxpayer may receive a refund after tax filing.

Does PCB reduce take-home pay?

Yes. PCB is deducted from monthly salary, so it reduces monthly take-home pay.

Can I calculate annual PCB?

Yes. This calculator estimates both monthly PCB and annual PCB deductions.

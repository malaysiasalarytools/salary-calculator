How Home Loan Repayment Is Calculated

A home loan calculator estimates monthly mortgage repayment based on property price, down payment, loan amount, interest rate and loan tenure. It is useful before buying a house, comparing loan packages or checking affordability.

Monthly Mortgage = P × r × (1 + r)n ÷ [(1 + r)n − 1] P is loan amount, r is monthly interest rate and n is total repayment months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this home loan calculator exact?

No. This calculator provides an estimate only. Actual bank installment may vary depending on interest rate, loan package, fees, insurance and approval conditions.

How much down payment do I need for a house in Malaysia?

A common simple estimate is 10% down payment, but the actual amount depends on margin of financing, property type, borrower profile and bank approval.

Does longer loan tenure reduce monthly installment?

Usually yes. A longer tenure may reduce monthly repayment, but it can increase total interest paid over the full loan period.

Should I check DSR before applying for a home loan?

Yes. DSR helps you estimate whether your monthly commitments are reasonable compared with your income.

Does this calculator include legal fees and stamp duty?

No. The main calculation focuses on loan repayment. You can enter a rough optional upfront cost estimate, but actual legal fees, stamp duty and other costs should be checked separately.

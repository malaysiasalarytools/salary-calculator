📌 MC Leave Quick Summary

Medical leave, often called MC leave or sick leave, is paid leave given when an employee is medically unfit for work and has a valid medical certificate.

What Is MC Leave?

MC leave is paid medical leave granted when an employee is sick or medically unfit to work. In Malaysia, employees are usually required to provide a valid medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner or approved medical institution.

Medical leave is different from annual leave because it is used for illness or medical reasons. It is also different from unpaid leave because eligible MC leave is normally paid.

Outpatient MC Leave vs Hospitalisation Leave

In Malaysia, ordinary outpatient MC leave and hospitalisation leave are usually treated differently. Outpatient MC leave commonly applies when an employee visits a clinic or doctor and is certified unfit for work, while hospitalisation leave applies when the employee requires hospital treatment or qualifying medical care.

Type of Medical Leave Common Entitlement Typical Situation
Outpatient MC Leave 14, 18 or 22 days per year depending on service period Clinic visit, illness, fever, infection or other medical condition certified by a doctor
Hospitalisation Leave Up to 60 days per year, where applicable Hospital admission, surgery, treatment or medical condition requiring hospital care

The exact treatment may depend on medical documents, company policy and the latest employment law requirements. Employees should submit the correct certificate or hospital documents so HR can record the leave properly.

How MC Leave Is Usually Applied

Employees usually need to inform their employer as soon as possible and submit a valid medical certificate. Employers may have internal procedures for reporting MC, uploading the certificate and updating payroll records.

MC Leave Used = Approved Medical Leave Days Taken Example: 2 approved MC days reduce the remaining yearly MC balance by 2 days

Documents Commonly Required for MC Leave

Employers usually require proper supporting documents before approving medical leave. This helps HR confirm that the absence is supported by a valid medical reason and record the leave correctly in payroll.

  • Medical certificate issued by a registered clinic, doctor or hospital
  • Hospitalisation certificate or admission record, where applicable
  • Discharge summary or supporting hospital documents, if requested
  • Company MC submission form or HR system upload, if required
  • Employee notification to supervisor or HR according to company policy

Some employers may require the original certificate, while others may accept a scanned copy or HR system upload first. Employees should follow internal procedures to avoid the absence being treated as unpaid or unauthorised.

Example MC Leave Situation

If an employee has worked for 3 years and is entitled to 18 days of paid sick leave per year:

  • Annual MC entitlement: 18 days
  • MC taken this month: 2 days
  • Remaining estimated MC balance: 16 days

Payroll treatment may depend on whether the MC is approved, properly documented and submitted according to company policy.

Hospitalisation Leave

Hospitalisation leave is usually treated separately from ordinary outpatient MC leave. It applies where an employee requires hospitalisation or medical treatment that qualifies under the relevant rules.

Employers may require supporting medical documents, hospital admission records or certificates before approving hospitalisation leave.

MC Leave During Probation

Employees on probation may still fall sick and may need to take medical leave. Whether the leave is paid and how it is recorded can depend on eligibility, employment terms and company policy.

A valid MC should be submitted even during probation. Taking genuine medical leave should not automatically mean an employee fails probation, but employers may still assess attendance, performance and overall suitability during the probation period.

MC Leave During Annual Leave

Employees sometimes become sick while they are already on annual leave. Whether the leave can be converted from annual leave to MC leave depends on company policy, medical documents and employer approval.

If this happens, the employee should inform HR or the supervisor as soon as possible and provide a valid medical certificate. Employers may review the timing, documents and internal policy before deciding how the leave should be recorded.

MC Leave During a Public Holiday

MC leave and public holidays are separate concepts. If an employee is sick on or around a public holiday, the payroll and leave treatment may depend on the actual date of the MC, the employee's work schedule and company policy.

For example, an MC issued for a normal working day after a public holiday may be recorded as medical leave. If the MC overlaps with a non-working public holiday, HR may need to review how the absence should be treated.

Common MC Leave Mistakes

Many MC leave disputes happen because employees and employers do not follow the same documentation or notification process. Clear HR policies can help reduce confusion and make payroll treatment more consistent.

  • Not informing the employer as soon as possible
  • Submitting the MC certificate late or not submitting it at all
  • Assuming a photo of the MC is enough without checking HR policy
  • Using annual leave when the absence should be medical leave
  • Taking sick leave without proper medical consultation or certification
  • Confusing outpatient MC leave with hospitalisation leave

Frequently Asked Questions

How many days of paid medical leave are employees entitled to in Malaysia?

Common sick leave entitlement is 14 days for less than 2 years of service, 18 days for 2 to 5 years and 22 days for more than 5 years, subject to eligibility and applicable employment rules.

Is MC leave paid in Malaysia?

Approved medical leave is normally paid for eligible employees when supported by a valid medical certificate and submitted according to company policy.

Can I take MC during probation?

Employees on probation may still need medical leave if they are genuinely unfit for work. The employee should notify the employer and submit a valid MC according to company policy. Payroll treatment may depend on eligibility, employment terms and internal HR rules.

Can an employer reject an MC?

Employers may review whether the MC is valid, properly issued and submitted according to company procedures. Disputes may depend on facts, documents and employment terms.

Can an employer verify an MC with the clinic?

Employers may want to verify whether a medical certificate is genuine if there are reasonable concerns. Verification should be handled carefully and professionally, taking into account privacy, company policy and the information reasonably needed to confirm the certificate.

Does MC leave affect annual leave?

MC leave and annual leave are normally separate leave categories. Approved medical leave should not usually reduce annual leave balance.

Can MC be taken during annual leave?

This depends on company policy and employer approval. If an employee becomes sick during annual leave, the employee should inform HR and provide a valid MC. The employer may decide whether the leave record can be changed from annual leave to medical leave.

Can MC be taken during a public holiday?

If the MC overlaps with a public holiday or non-working day, HR may need to review the work schedule, the MC date and company policy. The treatment may differ depending on whether the employee was scheduled to work on that day.

What happens if an employee takes MC without a certificate?

Without a valid certificate, the absence may be treated under company policy, such as unpaid leave, emergency leave or unauthorised absence.

Is hospitalisation leave the same as normal MC leave?

No. Hospitalisation leave is usually a separate entitlement and may require additional medical documents or hospital records.

Can an employee be terminated for taking MC?

Genuine medical leave supported by proper documents should be handled according to employment law, employment terms and company policy. However, repeated absence, fake MC, misuse of medical leave or failure to follow procedures may create disciplinary issues depending on the facts.

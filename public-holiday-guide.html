📌 Public Holiday Quick Summary

Public holidays are gazetted holidays observed in Malaysia. Employees may be entitled to paid holidays or additional pay depending on whether they are required to work.

Paid Holiday Eligible employees usually receive paid public holidays
Gazetted Public holidays may be national or state-specific
Replacement Substitute holidays may apply in certain situations
Overtime Working on holidays may affect overtime pay

What Are Public Holidays?

Public holidays are official holidays declared at national or state level. In Malaysia, some public holidays apply nationwide while others apply only in certain states.

Public holidays are important for payroll because they may affect paid leave, work schedules, replacement holidays and overtime calculations.

Types of Public Holidays in Malaysia

Public holidays in Malaysia can generally be grouped into national holidays and state holidays.

Holiday Type Applies To Simple Explanation
National Public Holiday Usually all Malaysia Examples include major nationwide holidays
State Public Holiday Specific states only Examples include state ruler birthdays or state-specific holidays
Replacement Holiday Depends on situation May apply when a holiday falls on a rest day or under company policy
Note: This is a simplified guide. Actual public holiday entitlement depends on gazetted holidays, state rules, employment terms and company policy.

Public Holiday Pay and Work

If an employee is not required to work on an applicable public holiday, the holiday is usually treated as paid time off for eligible employees. If the employee is required to work, additional pay or holiday work rates may apply.

Holiday Work Pay = Daily / Hourly Rate × Applicable Holiday Rate Example: Public holiday work may use a higher rate than normal work

Example Public Holiday Situation

If an employee is scheduled to work on a public holiday, payroll may need to consider:

  • Whether the holiday applies to the employee's state or workplace
  • Whether the employee is required to work
  • Whether additional pay, overtime or replacement holiday applies

The actual calculation should follow employment terms, payroll policy and applicable legal requirements.

National vs State Public Holidays

Not every public holiday applies to every employee in Malaysia. Some holidays are national, while others are state-specific. This means employees in different states may have different holiday calendars.

Businesses operating in multiple states should be careful when setting holiday schedules and payroll rules.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are public holidays paid in Malaysia?

Applicable public holidays are normally paid for eligible employees. The exact entitlement may depend on employment terms, location, holiday type and company policy.

Do all public holidays apply to every employee?

No. Some public holidays are national while others are state-specific. Employees should check whether the holiday applies to their workplace location.

What happens if an employee works on a public holiday?

Working on a public holiday may result in additional pay, overtime or other compensation depending on eligibility and applicable employment rules.

Is a replacement holiday required if a public holiday falls on a rest day?

Replacement holiday treatment may depend on the holiday, rest day arrangement, state rules, employment terms and company policy.

Can employers choose which public holidays to observe?

Employers must follow applicable legal requirements and employment terms. Some holidays may be mandatory while others may depend on the workplace calendar and policy.

Does public holiday work affect overtime calculation?

Yes. Work performed on a public holiday may use different rates compared with normal overtime, depending on eligibility and payroll rules.

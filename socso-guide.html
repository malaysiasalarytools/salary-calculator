What Is SOCSO?

SOCSO stands for Social Security Organisation, also known as PERKESO in Malaysia. It provides protection to eligible employees in cases such as employment injury, occupational disease, invalidity and death.

Unlike EPF, which focuses mainly on retirement savings, SOCSO is designed as a social protection scheme. It helps employees and their families receive financial support when unexpected work-related or health-related events occur.

🛡️ What Does SOCSO Cover?

Employment Injury

Protection for accidents that happen during work or while commuting for work purposes.

Invalidity

Support for employees who become unable to work due to serious illness or disability.

Medical Benefits

Medical treatment and related support may be provided for eligible SOCSO cases.

Dependants' Benefits

Financial support may be available to eligible dependants in certain cases.

How SOCSO Contributions Are Calculated

SOCSO is not usually calculated using a simple fixed percentage from salary. Instead, the contribution amount is selected from the SOCSO wage bracket table according to the employee's monthly wages.

Example SOCSO Calculation

If an employee earns RM4,000 per month, the estimated SOCSO contribution based on the simplified table may be:

  • Employee SOCSO: RM14.75
  • Employer SOCSO: RM51.65
  • Total SOCSO Contribution: RM66.40

The employee's portion is deducted from monthly salary, while the employer pays the employer portion separately.

Employee vs Employer SOCSO Contribution

In Malaysia payroll, SOCSO contributions involve two separate portions. The employee contribution is deducted from the employee's salary. The employer contribution is paid by the employer and is not deducted from the employee's take-home pay.

This means your net salary is affected only by the employee portion of SOCSO, together with other deductions such as EPF, EIS and PCB.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SOCSO contribution mandatory for employees in Malaysia?

SOCSO contribution is generally mandatory for eligible employees in Malaysia. Employers are responsible for registering eligible employees and making monthly SOCSO contributions through payroll.

How much SOCSO does an employee contribute every month?

The employee contribution depends on the employee's monthly wage bracket. SOCSO is usually based on a contribution table rather than a simple fixed percentage of salary.

How much SOCSO does an employer contribute?

The employer contribution is higher than the employee contribution and is paid separately by the employer. It is not deducted from the employee's take-home salary.

Is SOCSO calculated on basic salary or gross salary?

SOCSO is generally calculated based on wages that are subject to SOCSO contribution. Payroll treatment may depend on the type of payment, such as basic salary, allowances, overtime or other remuneration.

Does SOCSO affect take-home pay?

Yes. The employee portion of SOCSO is deducted from monthly salary, so it reduces take-home pay. The employer portion does not reduce the employee's net salary.

What happens if an employer does not pay SOCSO?

Employers are responsible for making SOCSO contributions for eligible employees. Failure to contribute may result in compliance issues, penalties or enforcement action by the relevant authority.

Is SOCSO the same as EIS?

No. SOCSO provides social security protection such as employment injury and invalidity benefits, while EIS focuses on employment insurance and support for eligible employees who lose their jobs.

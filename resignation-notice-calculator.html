How Resignation Notice Is Calculated

A notice period is the amount of time an employee normally continues working after submitting a resignation. The required notice period is usually stated in the employment contract or company policy.

Estimated Last Working Day = Resignation Date + Notice Period Use calendar days for day-count notice, or calendar months if your contract says one month, two months or three months notice.

Calendar Days vs Calendar Months

Some employment contracts mention a fixed number of days, while others mention calendar months. This difference can affect the final working day.

Resignation Date Notice Wording Estimated Last Working Day
1 January 7 days 8 January
1 January 30 days 31 January
15 January 1 calendar month 15 February
15 January 3 calendar months 15 April

Note: If your contract says “one calendar month”, it may not be the same as 30 days. Always check your employment contract and confirm with HR.

How Final Salary Is Estimated

If you leave before the end of the month, your final month salary may be prorated based on the number of days worked. If unused annual leave is allowed to be encashed, it may be added to your final salary.

Estimated Final Salary = Prorated Salary + Annual Leave Encashment Choose calendar days, actual working days or fixed 26 days depending on how your company prorates final salary.

Note: Final salary treatment can differ depending on employment contract, company policy, payroll cut-off, unpaid leave, deductions, allowances, claims, bonuses, annual leave encashment basis and HR approval.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I leave before my notice period ends?

It depends on your employment contract, employer approval and any agreement for early release, payment in lieu of notice or leave offset.

Is this calculator legally binding?

No. This calculator provides a simple estimate only. Always refer to your employment contract, HR policy and official confirmation from your employer.

What if my contract uses calendar months?

If your contract says one calendar month, two calendar months or three calendar months, use the calendar month option instead of the 30 / 60 / 90-day estimate.

Can annual leave be used during notice period?

It may be possible, but it usually depends on employer approval, company policy and employment contract terms.

Does public holiday extend notice period?

This depends on contract wording and company practice. Some notice periods are counted by calendar days, while others may be handled differently.

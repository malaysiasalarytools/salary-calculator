How EIS Is Calculated in Malaysia

EIS, also known as the Employment Insurance System or SIP, is an employment insurance contribution under PERKESO. It is normally calculated based on monthly wages, wage class and contribution rules. Employee EIS is deducted from salary, while employer EIS is paid by the employer on top of salary.

EIS Contribution = Monthly Wage × EIS Rate Example: RM4,000 × 0.2% = RM8 employee EIS deduction

Frequently Asked Questions

How is EIS calculated in Malaysia?

EIS is generally calculated based on monthly wages, with both employee and employer portions. This calculator uses a simplified 0.2% employee and 0.2% employer estimate.

Is employer EIS deducted from my salary?

No. Employee EIS is deducted from your salary. Employer EIS is an additional contribution paid by your employer on top of salary.

Does EIS affect take-home pay?

Yes. Employee EIS slightly reduces monthly take-home pay because it is deducted through payroll.

Is EIS the same as SOCSO?

No. EIS and SOCSO are different schemes, although both are related to PERKESO. EIS focuses on employment insurance, while SOCSO focuses on social security protection.

Is EIS the same as EPF?

No. EPF is mainly retirement savings. EIS is employment insurance protection and is usually much smaller than EPF contribution.

Why is my actual EIS different from this calculator?

Actual EIS may follow the official PERKESO contribution schedule, wage class, wage ceiling and payroll rounding rules, so it may differ from a simplified percentage estimate.

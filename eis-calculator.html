What is EIS in Malaysia?

The Employment Insurance System (EIS) is a social protection scheme administered by PERKESO. It provides temporary financial assistance and employment support services to employees who lose their jobs.

EIS was introduced to help workers maintain financial stability while searching for new employment opportunities.

Who Needs To Contribute To EIS?

Both employers and employees are generally required to contribute to EIS for eligible employment relationships.

Contributions are calculated based on monthly wages according to the official contribution schedule issued by PERKESO.

Benefits Provided Under EIS

  • Job Search Allowance
  • Reduced Income Allowance
  • Training Fee Assistance
  • Training Allowance
  • Employment Placement Services

How Is EIS Calculated?

EIS contributions are shared between employers and employees.

The contribution amount depends on monthly salary and contribution rates set by PERKESO.

Why Is EIS Important?

Unexpected job loss can create financial challenges for employees and their families.

EIS helps provide temporary income support while employees seek new employment opportunities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is EIS mandatory in Malaysia?

Yes, EIS is generally mandatory for eligible employees and employers.

Who manages EIS?

PERKESO administers the Employment Insurance System.

Can self-employed individuals contribute?

EIS mainly applies to employees working under contracts of service.

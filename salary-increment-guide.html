What Is a Salary Increment?

A salary increment is an increase in an employee's monthly salary. It may be given after annual appraisal, promotion, role expansion, market adjustment or successful salary negotiation.

When reviewing an increment, employees should look at both gross salary increase and net take-home pay after EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB deductions.

What Is a Typical Salary Increment in Malaysia?

Salary increments in Malaysia can vary widely depending on industry, company performance, employee performance, job level and market demand. Some employees receive annual salary adjustments, while others receive larger increments when they are promoted or when their role expands.

A typical salary increment should not be judged only by percentage. Employees should also consider whether the new salary is competitive for their role, whether responsibilities have increased and how the increment affects monthly take-home pay after deductions.

How Salary Increment Is Calculated

Salary increment can be calculated as either a fixed amount or a percentage of current salary. Percentage increment is often used during annual appraisal and promotion discussions.

New Salary = Current Salary × (1 + Increment %) Example: RM3,000 × 1.10 = RM3,300 after a 10% increment

Example Salary Increment Calculation

If your current salary is RM3,000 and your increment is 10%:

  • Current salary: RM3,000
  • Increment percentage: 10%
  • Increment amount: RM300
  • New salary: RM3,300

This is the gross salary increase before EPF, SOCSO, EIS, PCB and any other payroll deductions.

Salary Increment After Promotion

A promotion increment is usually linked to a higher role, new job grade, broader responsibilities or people management duties. In this situation, the increment should be assessed together with the new workload and expectations.

Employees should compare the promoted salary against market salary ranges, not only against their old salary. A promotion with a small increment may still leave the employee below market rate if the new role carries significantly higher responsibility.

Salary Increment vs Bonus

Salary increment and bonus are both forms of compensation, but they work differently. A salary increment increases monthly salary on a recurring basis, while a bonus is usually a one-off payment.

Item Salary Increment Bonus
Frequency Permanent monthly increase Usually one-off or periodic
Future Salary Raises future basic salary Does not usually change basic salary
Payroll Impact May affect EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB every month May affect payroll deductions when paid

Gross Increment vs Net Take-Home Pay

A salary increment increases gross salary, but your take-home pay may not increase by the full amount because statutory deductions and PCB may also increase.

For example, a RM500 gross increment does not always mean RM500 extra cash in your bank account after payroll deductions.

How Salary Increment Affects Income Tax

A salary increment increases annual income. This may increase monthly PCB deductions and final income tax payable, especially if the increment moves the employee into a higher taxable income range.

Employees should review both the gross increment and the net take-home impact. A higher salary is still beneficial, but the actual cash increase may be lower after EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB deductions.

Preparing for a Salary Increment Discussion

Before asking for a salary increment, employees should prepare clear evidence of performance, achievements and additional responsibilities. This makes the discussion more professional and less emotional.

  • List key achievements and completed projects
  • Show measurable results, KPIs or responsibilities handled
  • Research market salary for similar roles
  • Explain how your value to the company has increased
  • Consider both salary increment and career growth opportunities

Common Salary Increment Mistakes

Salary increment decisions should be reviewed carefully because a higher gross salary does not always translate into the same amount of extra cash every month.

  • Looking only at gross salary and ignoring net take-home pay
  • Forgetting that PCB and statutory deductions may increase
  • Comparing increment percentages without considering job level
  • Not researching market salary before negotiation
  • Focusing only on annual increment instead of long-term salary growth

Common Salary Increment Examples

Example 1: 5% Annual Increment

An employee earning RM4,000 receives a 5% increment. The gross salary increase is RM200, making the new salary RM4,200 before deductions.

Example 2: Promotion Increment

An employee is promoted to a senior role and receives a larger increment because the new role includes additional responsibilities, reporting duties or team supervision.

Example 3: Market Adjustment

A company adjusts an employee's salary to stay closer to market rate for similar roles. This may happen even without a formal promotion.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate salary increment percentage?

Subtract old salary from new salary, divide the result by old salary, then multiply by 100. For example, a RM300 increase from RM3,000 is a 10% salary increment.

What is a normal salary increment in Malaysia?

A normal salary increment depends on company performance, industry, job level and individual performance. Some employees receive small annual adjustments, while promotion or market adjustment may result in a larger increase.

Is a salary increment better than a bonus?

A salary increment is usually better for long-term income because it increases monthly salary permanently. A bonus can be useful, but it is usually a one-off payment and may not increase future basic salary.

How often do employees receive salary increments?

Many companies review salary annually, but the actual timing depends on company policy, appraisal cycle, promotion decisions and business performance.

Can I negotiate a salary increment?

Yes. Employees can discuss salary increments professionally by showing performance, achievements, increased responsibilities and market salary information.

Does salary increment increase EPF and PCB?

Yes. A higher salary may increase EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB deductions depending on salary level, contribution rules and tax position.

How much of an increment will increase my take-home pay?

The full gross increment may not become take-home pay because payroll deductions may also increase. Employees should compare net salary before and after increment.

Is a 10% salary increment good?

It depends on your role, industry, performance and current salary level. A 10% increment is generally meaningful for many employees, especially if it is not tied to a major increase in workload.

Can salary increment affect income tax?

Yes. A higher annual income may increase tax payable and monthly PCB deductions. The impact depends on total income, tax reliefs and rebates.

What Is a Salary Increment?

A salary increment is an increase in an employee's monthly salary. It may be given after annual appraisal, promotion, role expansion, market adjustment or successful salary negotiation.

When reviewing an increment, employees should look at both gross salary increase and net take-home pay after EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB deductions.

How Salary Increment Is Calculated

Salary increment can be calculated as either a fixed amount or a percentage of current salary.

New Salary = Current Salary × (1 + Increment %)Example: RM3,000 × 1.10 = RM3,300 after a 10% increment

Example Salary Increment Calculation

If your current salary is RM3,000 and your increment is 10%:

  • Current salary: RM3,000
  • Increment percentage: 10%
  • Increment amount: RM300
  • New salary: RM3,300

Gross Increment vs Net Take-Home Pay

A salary increment increases gross salary, but your take-home pay may not increase by the full amount because statutory deductions and PCB may also increase.

For example, a RM500 gross increment does not always mean RM500 extra cash in your bank account after payroll deductions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate salary increment percentage?

Subtract old salary from new salary, divide by old salary, then multiply by 100. For example, RM300 increase from RM3,000 is 10%.

Does salary increment increase EPF and PCB?

Yes. Higher salary may increase EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB deductions depending on salary level and tax position.

Is a 10% salary increment good?

It depends on your industry, role, performance, market salary and current pay level. A 10% increment is generally meaningful for many employees.

Should I compare increment by gross salary or net salary?

Use both. Gross salary shows official pay increase, while net salary shows actual monthly take-home impact.

Can salary increment affect income tax?

Yes. Higher annual income may increase tax payable and monthly PCB deductions.

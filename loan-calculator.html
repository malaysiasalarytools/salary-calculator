How Loan Repayment Is Calculated

A loan calculator helps estimate monthly repayment based on loan amount, interest rate and loan tenure. It is useful for comparing different loan options before applying for personal loans, car loans or housing loans.

Monthly Payment = P × r × (1 + r)n ÷ [(1 + r)n − 1] P is loan amount, r is monthly interest rate and n is total repayment months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this loan calculator exact?

No. This calculator provides an estimate only. Actual bank installment may vary depending on bank terms, fees, insurance, loan structure and approval conditions.

Can I use this for car loans?

Yes, it can provide a general estimate, but actual car loan calculations in Malaysia may use flat interest methods instead of reducing balance methods.

Can I use this for housing loans?

Yes. This calculator can estimate housing loan repayments using a reducing balance formula.

What affects monthly installment?

Loan amount, interest rate and loan tenure are the main factors affecting monthly installment.

Does longer tenure reduce monthly installment?

Usually yes, but longer tenure can increase total interest paid over the full loan period.

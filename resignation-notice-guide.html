What Is Resignation Notice?

Resignation notice is the period an employee must give before leaving employment. It gives the employer time to prepare handover, find replacement staff and complete payroll processing.

The notice period is usually stated in the employment contract or appointment letter.

Resignation During Probation

Employees can resign during probation, but the notice period may be different from confirmed employees. Some employment contracts provide a shorter probation notice period, while others use the same notice period for both probationary and confirmed employees.

Before resigning during probation, employees should check the offer letter, employment contract and company policy. Even during probation, it is better to resign in writing, confirm the final working day and complete any reasonable handover required by the employer.

What Is 24-Hour Resignation?

24-hour resignation means the employee leaves almost immediately. This may be allowed if the contract permits it or if the employee pays salary in lieu of notice. Otherwise, leaving without proper notice may create payroll or legal issues.

Salary In Lieu of Notice

Salary in lieu of notice is compensation paid when the required notice period is not fully served. This may happen when an employee wants to leave earlier than the contractual notice period, or when the employer agrees to release the employee before the notice period ends.

The amount is usually linked to the salary for the unserved notice period, but the exact calculation may depend on the employment contract, payroll policy and whether the calculation is based on calendar days, working days or another agreed method.

Salary In Lieu of Notice = Monthly Salary ÷ 26 × Notice Days This is a simplified example. Actual calculation may depend on contract and payroll policy.

Example Buy Out Notice Calculation

If monthly salary is RM3,900 and the employee needs to compensate 10 working days of notice:

  • Monthly salary: RM3,900
  • Estimated daily rate: RM3,900 ÷ 26 = RM150
  • Estimated compensation: RM150 × 10 days = RM1,500

This is only a simplified example and may differ based on company policy.

Final Salary After Resignation

After resignation, the final salary may include unpaid salary up to the last working day, approved overtime, allowances, commission, bonus entitlement if applicable, unused annual leave adjustment and any outstanding deductions.

Final salary can be lower than expected if there is salary in lieu of notice, unpaid leave, advance salary recovery, company loan deduction, missing handover items or other approved deductions. Employees should check the final payslip carefully and ask HR for a breakdown if anything is unclear.

Annual Leave During Notice Period

Employees often ask whether annual leave can be used during the notice period. This depends on employer approval, employment contract terms and company policy. Some employers allow employees to use annual leave to shorten the physical working period, while others require employees to serve the full notice period for handover purposes.

Unused annual leave may be paid out, offset against notice or adjusted in final salary depending on the employer's policy. Employees should confirm the arrangement in writing to avoid disputes over the last working day and final salary calculation.

Can Employers Reject Resignation?

In most cases, resignation is a notice given by the employee to end employment. An employer generally cannot force an employee to continue working indefinitely, but the employee should still comply with contractual notice obligations.

If the employee leaves without serving the required notice, the employer may claim salary in lieu of notice or make final salary adjustments according to the contract and applicable rules. To reduce risk, employees should submit resignation in writing and keep a copy of the resignation notice.

Resignation Checklist

A clear resignation process helps both employee and employer avoid payroll and handover disputes. Before leaving, employees should confirm the key items below.

  • Submit a written resignation letter or email
  • Confirm the required notice period and final working day
  • Discuss handover duties with supervisor or manager
  • Check annual leave balance and whether leave can be used or paid out
  • Return company property such as laptop, access card, uniform or tools
  • Review final salary, deductions and payslip details
  • Keep copies of resignation email, payslips and HR confirmation

Common Resignation Mistakes

Many resignation disputes happen because employees resign too quickly without checking the contract or final salary implications. Employers may also create confusion if they do not clearly explain the notice period, leave treatment or final salary calculation.

  • Resigning without checking the required notice period
  • Assuming 24-hour resignation is always allowed
  • Using annual leave during notice period without approval
  • Leaving before completing handover
  • Forgetting salary in lieu of notice or buy out implications
  • Not checking final salary, unused leave and deductions
  • Not keeping written proof of resignation and HR confirmation

Common Resignation Examples

Example 1: Resignation During Probation

An employee on probation has a 1-week notice period under the employment contract. If the employee submits resignation on Monday, the final working day will usually be based on the contract and company confirmation.

Example 2: Confirmed Employee With 1 Month Notice

A confirmed employee with a 1-month notice period should normally serve the notice period unless the employer agrees to an earlier release or the employee pays salary in lieu of notice.

Example 3: Employee Buys Out Notice

If an employee has 20 notice days remaining but wants to leave immediately, the employer may calculate salary in lieu of notice for the unserved period, subject to the contract and payroll policy.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much notice must I give when resigning in Malaysia?

The notice period usually follows the employment contract. Common periods include one week, one month, two months or three months depending on role, contract terms and company policy.

Can I resign with 24-hour notice?

This depends on the employment contract and employer agreement. If proper notice is not served, salary in lieu of notice may be required.

Can resignation notice be shortened?

Resignation notice may be shortened if the employer agrees to an earlier release or if the employee pays salary in lieu of the unserved notice period.

Can employers force employees to serve full notice?

Employers may require employees to follow the notice period stated in the contract. However, both parties may agree to an earlier release, leave offset or salary in lieu arrangement.

Can annual leave be used to offset notice period?

This depends on employer approval and company policy. Some employers allow leave offset, while others require full handover before the employee leaves.

What happens to unused annual leave after resignation?

Unused leave may be paid out, offset or adjusted in final salary depending on employment terms, company policy and HR approval.

When will final salary be paid after resignation?

Final salary timing depends on payroll cycle, company policy and any pending adjustments such as leave balance, notice shortfall, deductions or company property return.

Can employer reject resignation?

An employer generally cannot force an employee to continue working indefinitely, but the employee should comply with notice obligations or salary in lieu requirements.

Can resignation be withdrawn after submission?

Withdrawal of resignation usually requires employer agreement. Once resignation has been accepted or processed, the employer may not be required to accept a withdrawal.

Can employers terminate employment during notice period?

This depends on the facts, contract terms and employment rules. Employers and employees should handle any early release, misconduct issue or termination during notice carefully and in writing.

Is resignation during probation allowed?

Yes. Employees can resign during probation but should follow the probation notice period stated in the contract or appointment letter.

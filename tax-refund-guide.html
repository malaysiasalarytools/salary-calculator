What Is A Tax Refund?

A tax refund occurs when the total tax paid during the year is higher than the actual tax payable after tax assessment.

When this happens, the excess amount may be refunded to the taxpayer.

Why Do Tax Refunds Happen?

Tax refunds commonly occur because PCB deductions made during the year are higher than the final tax liability.

Other reasons may include:

  • Additional tax relief claims
  • Changes in income during the year
  • Bonus payments affecting tax calculations
  • Incorrect tax estimates during payroll processing
  • Eligible rebates and deductions

What Is PCB?

PCB stands for Potongan Cukai Bulanan, which is Malaysia's monthly tax deduction system.

Employers deduct PCB from employee salaries and remit it to the tax authority throughout the year.

PCB vs Actual Tax Payable

PCB is only an estimate of income tax.

The actual tax payable is determined when annual income, tax reliefs and deductions are taken into account.

If PCB paid exceeds actual tax payable, a refund may be generated.

Example Tax Refund Calculation

Assume an employee paid RM4,500 in PCB during the year.

After filing the annual tax return, actual tax payable is calculated at RM3,800.

Estimated tax refund:

RM4,500 − RM3,800 = RM700

The taxpayer may be entitled to a RM700 refund.

Common Tax Reliefs That May Increase Refunds

  • Personal Relief
  • EPF Contributions
  • Life Insurance
  • Medical Expenses
  • Education Expenses
  • Lifestyle Relief
  • Child Relief
  • Spouse Relief

Claiming eligible tax reliefs may reduce chargeable income and increase potential refunds.

How To Maximise Tax Refund Legally

  • Keep all receipts and supporting documents
  • Claim eligible tax reliefs accurately
  • Review PCB deductions regularly
  • File tax returns on time
  • Ensure personal details are updated correctly

Does Everyone Receive A Tax Refund?

No.

Some taxpayers may receive a refund, while others may need to pay additional tax if PCB deducted was insufficient.

How Long Does A Tax Refund Take?

Processing times may vary depending on filing method, tax authority procedures and completeness of information submitted.

Taxpayers should ensure banking details and filing information are accurate to avoid delays.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did I receive a tax refund?

You may have paid more tax through PCB deductions than your actual tax payable.

Can I get a refund every year?

Not necessarily. Refunds depend on your annual tax position and deductions claimed.

Can tax reliefs increase refunds?

Yes. Eligible tax reliefs may reduce chargeable income and potentially increase refunds.

What if I owe additional tax?

If actual tax payable exceeds PCB paid, you may need to pay the difference.

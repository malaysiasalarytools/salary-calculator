How Annual Leave Is Calculated in Malaysia

Annual leave is paid leave that employees may take during the year while still receiving salary. In Malaysia, minimum annual leave entitlement commonly depends on the employee's length of service.

Pro-Rated Leave = Annual Entitlement × Months Worked ÷ 12 Example: 12 days × 6 months ÷ 12 = 6 days

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this annual leave calculator exact?

No. This calculator provides an estimate only. Actual entitlement may depend on employment contract, company policy and applicable law.

How many annual leave days do employees get in Malaysia?

A common minimum annual leave reference is 8 days for less than 2 years of service, 12 days for 2 to 5 years, and 16 days for more than 5 years.

Can annual leave be pro-rated?

Yes. Annual leave is often pro-rated when an employee joins or leaves during the year, depending on employment contract, company policy and applicable rules.

Can annual leave be taken during notice period?

It may be possible, but it usually depends on employer approval, employment contract and company policy.

Can unused annual leave be encashed?

It depends on the employment contract, company policy and situation. Some employers may allow encashment, especially when employment ends.

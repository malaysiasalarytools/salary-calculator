How EPF Is Calculated in Malaysia

EPF, also known as KWSP, is usually calculated based on your monthly wages and the applicable employee and employer contribution rates. Employee EPF is deducted from salary, while employer EPF is paid by the employer on top of salary.

EPF Contribution = Monthly Salary × EPF Rate Example: RM4,000 × 11% = RM440 employee EPF deduction

Frequently Asked Questions

How is EPF calculated in Malaysia?

EPF is generally calculated based on monthly wages and the applicable employee and employer contribution rates. This calculator uses percentage-based estimates for easier planning.

Is employer EPF deducted from my salary?

No. Employee EPF is deducted from your salary. Employer EPF is an additional contribution paid by your employer on top of your salary.

Why is my actual EPF different from this calculator?

Actual EPF may follow the official KWSP contribution schedule, wage category and payroll rounding rules, so it may differ from a simple percentage calculation.

Does EPF affect take-home pay?

Yes. Employee EPF reduces your monthly take-home pay because it is deducted from your salary, but the amount goes into your EPF retirement savings account.

Does this calculator include SOCSO, EIS and PCB?

No. This page focuses on EPF only. To estimate EPF together with SOCSO, EIS and PCB, use the full Malaysia salary calculator.

What is the common employer EPF rate in Malaysia?

For Malaysian citizens and permanent residents below age 60, a common employer EPF rate is 13% for monthly wages of RM5,000 and below, and 12% for monthly wages above RM5,000.

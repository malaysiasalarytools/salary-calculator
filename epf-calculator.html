EPF Contribution = Monthly Salary × EPF Rate Example: RM4,000 × 11% = RM440 employee EPF deduction

Employee EPF

Employee EPF is deducted from your monthly salary. This amount reduces your take-home pay, but it is credited into your EPF retirement savings account.

Employer EPF

Employer EPF is paid by your employer on top of your salary. It is part of your employment cost and contributes to your EPF account.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is EPF calculated in Malaysia?

EPF is generally calculated based on monthly wages and applicable employee and employer contribution rates. This calculator uses percentage-based estimates for easier planning.

Is employer EPF deducted from my salary?

No. Employee EPF is deducted from your salary. Employer EPF is an additional contribution paid by your employer on top of your salary.

Why is my actual EPF different from this calculator?

Actual EPF may follow the official KWSP contribution schedule, wage category and payroll rounding rules, so it may differ from a simple percentage calculation.

Does EPF affect take-home pay?

Yes. Employee EPF reduces your monthly take-home pay, but it goes into your EPF retirement savings account.

Does this calculator include SOCSO, EIS and PCB?

This page focuses on EPF only. To estimate EPF together with SOCSO, EIS and PCB, use the full Malaysia salary calculator.

📌 Employment Contract Quick Summary

An employment contract sets out the key terms between employer and employee, including role, salary, working hours, leave, notice period and benefits.

Salary Basic pay and allowances
Probation Trial period terms
Notice Resignation or termination notice
Benefits Leave, claims and payroll items

What Is an Employment Contract?

An employment contract is an agreement between employer and employee that explains the main terms of employment. It may appear as an offer letter, appointment letter or formal employment agreement.

This guide is written as a practical overview for employees, job seekers, HR staff and small business owners. Actual treatment may depend on employment contract terms, company policy and the latest legal requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is employment contract in Malaysia?

Employment contract refers to an employment topic that may affect salary, leave, working conditions or payroll treatment depending on the contract and company policy.

Is employment contract covered by company policy?

Many employers cover this in the employment contract, employee handbook or HR policy. Employees should check written terms first.

Can employer and employee agree on different terms?

Some terms may be agreed in the contract, but they should not conflict with applicable minimum legal requirements.

Does employment contract affect salary calculation?

It may affect salary, deductions, leave balance or final pay depending on the situation and payroll treatment.

What should employees check first?

Employees should check their offer letter, employment contract, company policy and written HR communication.

What if there is a dispute?

For disputes, employees should keep records and consider contacting the relevant authority or obtaining professional advice.

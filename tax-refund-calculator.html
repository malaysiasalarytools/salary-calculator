How Tax Refund Is Calculated in Malaysia

A tax refund happens when the total tax already paid is more than the actual income tax payable. In Malaysia, many employees pay tax through monthly PCB deductions. During annual tax filing, the final tax payable may be lower or higher than the total PCB already deducted.

Tax Refund = Total PCB Paid − Actual Income Tax Payable If the result is positive, you may have a refund. If negative, you may have additional tax payable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this tax refund calculator exact?

No. This calculator provides an estimate only. Actual refund depends on LHDN assessment, tax reliefs, rebates, zakat and filing details.

What is PCB?

PCB is Malaysia's monthly tax deduction system where employers deduct tax from salary every month.

When do I get a tax refund?

A refund may arise when total tax already paid is higher than final tax payable after annual tax filing.

What if my result is negative?

A negative result means your actual tax payable is higher than PCB paid, so you may need to pay additional tax.

Can tax relief increase my refund?

Yes. If eligible tax reliefs reduce your actual tax payable below your PCB paid, your refund estimate may increase.

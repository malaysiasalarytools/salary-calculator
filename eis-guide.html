What Is EIS?

The Employment Insurance System (EIS), also known as Sistem Insurans Pekerjaan (SIP), is a Malaysian employment insurance scheme administered by PERKESO. It is designed to support eligible employees who lose their jobs.

EIS is different from EPF and SOCSO. EPF focuses mainly on retirement savings, SOCSO focuses on social security and employment injury protection, while EIS focuses on job loss protection and re-employment support.

How EIS Contributions Are Calculated

EIS contributions are shared equally between employee and employer. Each side commonly contributes 0.2% of the employee's assumed monthly salary, subject to the official EIS contribution table and wage ceiling.

EIS Contribution = 0.2% Employee + 0.2% Employer Total EIS contribution is commonly 0.4% of the assumed monthly salary.

Does EIS Affect Take-Home Pay?

Yes. The employee portion of EIS is deducted from monthly salary, so it slightly reduces take-home pay. The employer portion is paid separately by the employer and does not reduce the employee's net salary.

Although the EIS deduction is usually small compared with EPF and PCB, it is still part of the total statutory deductions used when calculating monthly net salary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is EIS contribution mandatory for employees in Malaysia?

EIS contribution is generally mandatory for eligible employees in Malaysia. Employers are responsible for deducting the employee portion and paying the employer portion through monthly payroll.

How much EIS does an employee contribute every month?

The employee contribution is commonly 0.2% of the assumed monthly salary, subject to the official EIS contribution table and wage ceiling.

How much EIS does an employer contribute?

The employer also commonly contributes 0.2% of the employee's assumed monthly salary. This employer portion is paid separately and is not deducted from the employee's take-home salary.

Is EIS calculated on basic salary or gross salary?

EIS is generally calculated based on wages that are subject to EIS contribution. Payroll treatment may depend on the type of payment, such as basic salary, fixed allowances, overtime or other remuneration.

Does EIS reduce take-home pay?

Yes. The employee portion of EIS is deducted from monthly salary, so it slightly reduces monthly take-home pay. The employer portion does not reduce the employee's net salary.

Is EIS the same as SOCSO?

No. SOCSO provides social security protection such as employment injury and invalidity benefits. EIS focuses on job loss protection, financial assistance and re-employment support.

Can EIS help if I lose my job?

Eligible employees may apply for EIS benefits after job loss, subject to PERKESO rules, qualifying conditions and approval. Benefits may include financial assistance and re-employment support.

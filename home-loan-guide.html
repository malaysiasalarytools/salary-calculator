What Is a Home Loan?

A home loan is financing used to purchase residential property. The borrower repays the loan through monthly instalments over a long tenure, usually with interest.

Because a home loan is a long-term commitment, it is important to understand instalment, down payment, interest rate, lock-in terms and how the repayment affects your salary cash flow.

🧾

Fees & Costs

How Home Loan Affordability Works

Home loan affordability depends on income, existing debts, loan amount, tenure and interest rate. A property may look affordable from the selling price, but the monthly instalment and upfront cash cost must also be considered.

Home Loan Budget = Instalment + Existing Debts + Living ExpensesUse salary cash flow to test whether the repayment is sustainable.

Example Home Loan Planning

Assume a borrower earns RM6,000 per month and estimates a home loan instalment of RM1,800 with existing debts of RM600.

  • Monthly income: RM6,000
  • Home loan instalment: RM1,800
  • Existing commitments: RM600
  • Total debt commitments: RM2,400

The borrower should also check living expenses, emergency savings and maintenance costs before committing.

Home Loan Checklist

Check DSR

Estimate whether the new instalment fits your income and debts.

Prepare Cash

Budget for down payment, legal fees and other upfront costs.

Compare Rates

Small interest differences can matter over a long tenure.

Stress Test

Consider whether you can still pay if expenses increase.

Planning Note: Do not only look at the bank instalment. Ownership costs such as maintenance, insurance, quit rent and assessment can affect monthly budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

What affects home loan affordability?

Income, existing debts, loan amount, interest rate, tenure, credit profile and property details may all affect affordability and approval.

Is monthly instalment the only cost of buying a house?

No. You should also plan for down payment, legal fees, valuation, insurance, maintenance and other ownership costs.

Can a higher salary improve home loan chances?

A higher salary can help, but lenders may also consider DSR, employment stability, credit record and existing commitments.

Should I take the longest home loan tenure?

A longer tenure can reduce monthly instalment but may increase total interest paid. Choose a tenure that balances cash flow and total cost.

Can I apply for a home loan with existing debts?

Yes, but existing debts increase DSR and may reduce borrowing capacity.

What Is Overtime Pay?

Overtime pay is extra compensation for eligible employees who work beyond their normal working hours. It is commonly calculated based on the employee's ordinary rate of pay and the applicable overtime multiplier.

In Malaysia, overtime rules may depend on employment coverage, salary level, working arrangement, employment contract and applicable labour law requirements.

Who Is Eligible for Overtime Pay?

Not all employees are automatically entitled to overtime pay. Eligibility may depend on employment coverage, job responsibilities, salary level and applicable employment law requirements.

Employees should review their employment contract and company policies to understand whether overtime payments apply to their position.

How Overtime Pay Is Calculated

Overtime is usually calculated using the employee's hourly rate and the applicable overtime multiplier. The hourly rate may be based on ordinary rate of pay according to the payroll method used.

Overtime Pay = Hourly Rate × Overtime Hours × Multiplier Example: RM15 × 4 hours × 1.5 = RM90 overtime pay

Example Overtime Calculation

If an employee's hourly rate is RM15 and the employee works 4 overtime hours on an ordinary working day:

  • Hourly rate: RM15
  • Overtime hours: 4 hours
  • Multiplier: 1.5x
  • Estimated overtime pay: RM90

Different rates may apply for rest days, public holidays or special working arrangements.

Ordinary Day, Rest Day and Public Holiday Overtime

Overtime is not always paid at the same rate. Work performed after normal hours on a normal working day is usually treated differently from work performed on a rest day or public holiday.

Employees should check their employment contract and company payroll policy to understand how different overtime situations are recorded and paid.

Overtime on Rest Days

Work performed on a designated rest day is commonly treated differently from ordinary working day overtime.

Different overtime multipliers and payment rules may apply depending on the number of hours worked and the employee's employment arrangement.

Overtime on Public Holidays

Employees who work on public holidays may be entitled to higher overtime rates compared to ordinary working days.

Actual calculations may depend on company policy, employment terms and applicable employment law requirements.

How Overtime Is Usually Approved

Many employers require overtime to be approved before payment is processed.

  • Employee works overtime
  • Manager reviews and approves hours
  • HR verifies records
  • Payroll calculates overtime pay
  • Payment is included in salary

Overtime Pay vs Replacement Leave

Some employers may offer replacement leave instead of overtime pay for certain situations.

Replacement leave allows employees to take time off in exchange for additional hours worked, subject to company policy and approval requirements.

Does Overtime Affect EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB?

Overtime pay may increase an employee's gross income. Depending on payroll treatment and applicable contribution rules, overtime payments may affect EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB calculations.

Employees should review their payslip and payroll records to understand how overtime payments are reflected.

Common Overtime Mistakes

Overtime disputes often occur because overtime hours are not properly recorded or approved.

  • Failing to record overtime hours
  • Assuming all employees qualify for overtime
  • Missing approval requirements
  • Submitting claims after payroll cut-off dates
  • Confusing overtime pay with allowances

Frequently Asked Questions

How is overtime pay calculated in Malaysia?

Overtime pay is commonly calculated using hourly rate multiplied by overtime hours and the applicable multiplier, such as 1.5x for ordinary working day overtime.

What is the common overtime rate for a normal working day?

A common overtime rate for eligible ordinary working day overtime is 1.5 times the hourly rate, subject to eligibility and applicable employment rules.

Is public holiday overtime higher than normal overtime?

Public holiday overtime is commonly paid at a higher rate than ordinary working day overtime. The actual calculation depends on applicable rules and company payroll policy.

Are all employees entitled to overtime pay?

Not necessarily. Overtime entitlement may depend on employment coverage, salary level, job category, working arrangement and employment contract terms.

Can an employer require approval before paying overtime?

Many employers require overtime to be pre-approved or properly recorded before payment. Employees should follow company procedures for overtime claims.

Can overtime be replaced with time off instead of payment?

Some employers may offer replacement leave instead of overtime payment depending on company policy and employment arrangements. Employees should confirm the applicable arrangement with their employer.

Can part-time employees receive overtime pay?

Overtime entitlement for part-time employees may depend on employment terms, working hours and applicable employment requirements. Employees should review their employment contract for details.

Can employers limit overtime hours?

Employers commonly manage overtime through scheduling, approval procedures and operational requirements. Employees should follow company policies regarding overtime arrangements.

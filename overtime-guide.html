What Is Overtime Pay?

Overtime pay is additional pay given when an employee works beyond normal working hours.

Overtime may happen when employees work extra hours on normal working days, rest days or public holidays. The applicable overtime rate may depend on employment terms, working schedule and applicable employment rules.

How Is Overtime Calculated?

A common simplified method is to first calculate the employee's hourly rate, then multiply it by the overtime rate and overtime hours.

The simplified formula is:

Overtime Pay = Hourly Rate × Overtime Rate × Overtime Hours

How To Calculate Hourly Rate

Hourly rate is commonly estimated by dividing monthly salary by working days and working hours per day.

A simple formula is:

Hourly Rate = Monthly Salary ÷ 26 ÷ 8

For example, if monthly salary is RM3,000:

RM3,000 ÷ 26 ÷ 8 = RM14.42 per hour.

Common Overtime Rates

Overtime rates may vary depending on whether the work is performed on a normal working day, rest day or public holiday.

A common basic overtime estimate for normal working days is 1.5 times the hourly rate.

  • Normal working day overtime: commonly estimated at 1.5x hourly rate
  • Rest day overtime: may follow different rules
  • Public holiday overtime: may follow different rules

Example Overtime Calculation

Assume an employee earns RM3,000 per month and works 10 overtime hours.

Estimated hourly rate:

RM3,000 ÷ 26 ÷ 8 = RM14.42

Estimated OT rate at 1.5x:

RM14.42 × 1.5 = RM21.63

Estimated overtime pay:

RM21.63 × 10 hours = RM216.30

Why Overtime Calculation Matters

Correct overtime calculation is important because it affects employee take-home pay and payroll cost.

For employees, understanding overtime pay helps estimate total monthly income. For employers, it helps manage payroll cost and avoid payroll mistakes.

Overtime And Payslip

Overtime pay should usually be shown clearly in payroll records or payslips so that employees can understand how their salary is calculated.

A payslip may include basic salary, overtime pay, EPF, SOCSO, EIS, PCB and net salary.

Overtime And Tax

Overtime pay may increase total monthly income. Higher income may affect PCB deduction or annual income tax calculation.

Employees who regularly receive overtime pay may want to review their annual income and tax position.

Tips For Employees

  • Track overtime hours worked
  • Keep records of approved overtime
  • Check payslip details every month
  • Understand your hourly rate
  • Refer to your employment contract or HR policy

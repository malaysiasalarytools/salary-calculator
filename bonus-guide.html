What Is a Bonus?

A bonus is an extra payment given to an employee in addition to monthly salary. In Malaysia, bonuses are commonly paid as annual bonus, performance bonus, contractual bonus, discretionary bonus or festival-related bonus.

Bonus is important because it can significantly affect annual income. When comparing job offers, employees should not only compare monthly salary, but also consider whether the employer offers contractual bonus, variable bonus or no guaranteed bonus.

Common Types of Bonus in Malaysia

Bonus arrangements can differ between companies. The most important point is whether the bonus is guaranteed under the employment contract or only paid at the employer's discretion.

📄

Contractual Bonus

Bonus stated in the offer letter or employment contract, usually with clearer entitlement terms.

🎯

Performance Bonus

Bonus linked to employee performance, KPI achievement or appraisal results.

🏢

Company Bonus

Bonus based on company profit, management decision or business performance.

🎁

Discretionary Bonus

Bonus that depends on employer decision and may not be guaranteed every year.

Annual Bonus vs Performance Bonus

Annual bonus and performance bonus are often used interchangeably, but they may work differently depending on the employer's policy. An annual bonus is commonly linked to the company's yearly results or general bonus cycle, while a performance bonus is more closely connected to individual KPI, appraisal rating or department targets.

Employees should check whether the bonus is based on a fixed number of months, a percentage of salary, a performance score or management discretion. Understanding the bonus basis helps employees compare salary packages more accurately.

Bonus Type Common Basis What Employees Should Check
Annual Bonus Company yearly results or annual bonus cycle Whether it is guaranteed, discretionary or linked to company performance
Performance Bonus Individual KPI, appraisal rating or target achievement How performance is measured and when the bonus is paid
Contractual Bonus Written employment contract or offer letter Exact entitlement, conditions and payment timing

How Bonus Is Calculated

Bonus is often calculated based on monthly salary. For example, a one-month bonus usually means the employee receives an additional amount equal to one month of salary. Some employers may calculate bonus based on basic salary only, while others may use gross salary or a different formula.

Bonus Amount = Monthly Salary × Bonus Months Example: RM4,000 × 1 month = RM4,000 bonus

Example Bonus Calculation

If an employee earns RM4,000 per month and receives a 1.5-month bonus, the estimated bonus may be:

  • Monthly salary: RM4,000
  • Bonus months: 1.5 months
  • Estimated bonus: RM6,000

Actual take-home bonus may be lower after statutory deductions and tax treatment, depending on payroll calculation and employee tax situation.

Contractual Bonus vs Discretionary Bonus

Contractual bonus and discretionary bonus are very different. A contractual bonus is usually written in the employment contract or offer letter. A discretionary bonus is usually not guaranteed and depends on employer decision, company performance or internal policy.

Bonus Type Simple Meaning Usually Guaranteed?
Contractual Bonus Bonus stated in employment contract or offer letter Usually yes, subject to terms
Discretionary Bonus Bonus decided by employer based on policy or performance Usually no
Performance Bonus Bonus linked to KPI, appraisal or target achievement Depends on scheme
Career Note: When comparing job offers, a higher monthly salary is usually more stable than a non-guaranteed bonus. However, a strong bonus scheme can make total annual compensation much more attractive.

Does Bonus Affect EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB?

Bonus may affect payroll deductions and tax calculation. Depending on how the bonus is treated in payroll, it may increase monthly statutory deductions and PCB for that month. Employees should expect bonus months to have different deductions compared with normal salary months.

Bonus After Tax and Deductions

Many employees focus on the gross bonus amount, but the actual take-home bonus may be lower after payroll deductions. Bonus payments may affect EPF contributions, PCB tax deductions and other salary-related calculations for the month.

A higher bonus month may therefore show higher deductions on the payslip compared with a normal salary month. Employees should compare gross bonus, net bonus and total annual income before deciding how much the bonus really improves their cash flow.

Bonus vs Salary Increment

A bonus can increase annual income, but it does not usually increase monthly basic salary unless it is converted into a permanent salary adjustment. A salary increment, on the other hand, increases future monthly salary and may also affect future EPF, PCB, overtime rate and salary-based benefits.

Item Bonus Salary Increment
Payment Nature Usually one-off or periodic Permanent increase in monthly salary
Monthly Salary Usually no change Higher monthly salary going forward
Annual Income Can increase annual income Increases annual income every year if maintained
Long-Term Impact Depends on whether bonus continues Usually stronger long-term income impact

Bonus During Probation

Bonus entitlement during probation depends on the employment contract, bonus policy and the timing of the bonus period. Some employers exclude probationary employees from bonus schemes, while others may allow pro-rated bonus if the employee meets the required conditions.

Employees should check whether the bonus policy requires confirmation, minimum service period or active employment on the payment date. If the bonus is discretionary, the employer may have more flexibility in deciding whether probation employees are included.

Bonus and Resignation

Employees who resign before bonus payout often ask whether they are still entitled to bonus. The answer depends on whether the bonus is contractual or discretionary, whether the employee has completed the required service period and whether the bonus policy requires the employee to be actively employed on the payment date.

If the bonus is clearly contractual, the employment terms should be reviewed carefully. If the bonus is discretionary, employers usually have more room to decide based on policy, performance and company practice.

Common Bonus Mistakes

Bonus misunderstandings usually happen because employees compare only the headline bonus amount without checking contract terms, tax impact or whether the bonus is guaranteed.

  • Assuming a discretionary bonus is guaranteed every year
  • Comparing only monthly salary instead of total annual compensation
  • Ignoring PCB and payroll deductions in the bonus month
  • Not checking whether bonus is based on basic salary or gross salary
  • Assuming probation employees automatically receive bonus
  • Resigning before checking bonus payment conditions

Bonus on Payslip

Bonus is usually shown separately on the payslip as additional income, bonus payment or incentive. The payslip may also show higher deductions for the month if bonus affects statutory contributions or tax deduction.

Employees should keep bonus payslips for income tax filing, loan applications and personal financial records because bonus can affect annual income proof.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is bonus compulsory in Malaysia?

Bonus is not automatically compulsory unless it is stated in the employment contract, offer letter, collective agreement or company policy that creates a clear entitlement.

What is the difference between contractual bonus and discretionary bonus?

Contractual bonus is usually promised in writing and may be treated as part of employment terms. Discretionary bonus depends on employer decision and is usually not guaranteed every year.

How do I calculate a one-month bonus?

A one-month bonus is commonly calculated as one month of salary. For example, if your monthly salary is RM4,000, a one-month bonus is RM4,000 before any applicable deductions.

Does bonus affect PCB tax?

Yes. Bonus may increase taxable income and can result in higher PCB deduction for the bonus month, depending on payroll calculation and the employee's tax position.

Does bonus affect EPF contribution?

Bonus may be subject to EPF depending on the nature of payment and payroll treatment. Employees should check payslip details or confirm with HR if unsure.

Should I compare jobs by monthly salary or annual income?

It is better to compare total annual income, including monthly salary, guaranteed bonus, allowances and benefits. Monthly salary is more stable, but guaranteed bonus can increase annual compensation.

Can a company choose not to pay bonus?

If the bonus is discretionary, the company may decide whether to pay it based on performance, profit or policy. If the bonus is contractual, the employment terms should be reviewed carefully.

Can employees receive bonus during probation?

This depends on the employment contract and bonus policy. Some employers may exclude probation employees, while others may provide pro-rated or discretionary bonus if the employee meets the conditions.

Can bonus be paid monthly?

Some companies may pay incentives, commissions or variable bonuses monthly, but annual bonus is commonly paid once a year or after performance review. The exact timing depends on company policy.

Can employers cancel a bonus?

If the bonus is discretionary, employers may decide not to pay it depending on performance, profit or policy. If the bonus is contractual, the written employment terms should be reviewed carefully.

Is bonus taxable in Malaysia?

Bonus may form part of taxable employment income and may affect PCB deduction for the month. Employees should review their EA Form and tax filing records.

Does bonus affect loan applications?

Bonus may help show higher annual income, but banks may treat fixed salary and variable bonus differently. Keeping bonus payslips and EA Forms can help support income verification.

Should I compare jobs based on salary or total compensation?

It is better to compare total compensation, including monthly salary, guaranteed bonus, allowances, benefits and long-term increment potential.

What Is a Payslip?

A payslip is a document provided by an employer to show how an employee's monthly salary is calculated. It usually includes salary, allowances, overtime, statutory deductions and the final amount paid to the employee.

For employees, payslips are useful for checking salary accuracy, applying for loans, filing income tax and keeping personal financial records.

Gross Pay vs Net Pay

Gross pay is the total salary amount before deductions. It may include basic salary, allowances, overtime, bonus, commission or other approved additions paid through payroll.

Net pay is the final amount received by the employee after payroll deductions. Employees should compare both gross pay and net pay because the salary stated in an offer letter may not be the same as the actual cash received in the bank account.

How to Read Your Payslip

Start by checking your gross pay, then review each deduction. Your net pay should normally equal gross salary plus additions minus statutory and other approved deductions.

Net Pay = Gross Pay + Additions − DeductionsDeductions may include EPF, SOCSO, EIS, PCB, unpaid leave or other approved deductions.

Example Payslip Breakdown

  • Basic salary: RM4,000
  • Allowance: RM200
  • Gross pay: RM4,200
  • EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB: deducted through payroll
  • Net pay: salary after total deductions

Bonus and Overtime on Payslips

Payslips may look different in months where bonus, overtime, commission or incentives are paid. These additional payments can increase gross income and may also increase deductions or PCB for that month.

Employees should check whether bonus and overtime are shown clearly as separate payslip items. This makes it easier to verify the amount paid, understand tax treatment and keep records for future reference.

Payslips for Loan Applications

Payslips are commonly requested when applying for home loans, personal loans, car loans or credit facilities. Banks and lenders use payslips to verify monthly income, employment stability and the consistency of salary payments.

Employees who receive variable income such as overtime, commission or bonus should keep several months of payslips because lenders may review average income instead of relying on one month only.

Payslip vs EA Form

Payslips and EA Forms are both important salary records, but they serve different purposes. Payslips show monthly salary details, while the EA Form summarises annual employment income for tax filing.

Common Payslip Mistakes

Payslip errors and misunderstandings can happen when employees only look at the final net pay without reviewing the full breakdown. Checking each component helps avoid confusion and payroll disputes.

  • Ignoring payroll deductions and only checking net pay
  • Not checking overtime, bonus or allowance entries
  • Assuming net pay is the same as salary package
  • Not keeping payslips for tax filing or loan applications
  • Not asking HR when deductions look unusual
  • Confusing monthly payslip amounts with annual EA Form totals

Common Payslip Examples

Example 1: Basic Salary Only

An employee receives only basic salary for the month. The payslip should show basic salary, statutory deductions and final net pay.

Example 2: Salary Plus Overtime

An employee works approved overtime during the month. The payslip should show overtime as an additional income item, and deductions may be higher because gross pay has increased.

Example 3: Salary Plus Bonus

In a bonus month, the payslip may show normal salary and bonus separately. PCB may be higher for that month depending on payroll calculation.

Example 4: Salary Plus Allowance

If an employee receives a transport, meal or fixed allowance, the payslip should show whether the allowance is included as part of gross pay and how it affects deductions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should be included in a payslip?

A payslip usually includes employee details, pay period, basic salary, allowances, overtime, bonus, statutory deductions, other deductions and net salary.

Why is my net pay lower than my gross salary?

Net pay is lower because deductions such as EPF, SOCSO, EIS, PCB or other approved deductions are subtracted from gross salary.

Can employers issue electronic payslips?

Many employers provide payslips electronically through payroll systems or HR portals. Employees should make sure they can access and save copies for future reference.

How long should payslips be kept?

Employees should keep payslips for tax filing, loan applications, salary disputes and personal records. Keeping digital copies makes it easier to retrieve documents when needed.

Can payslips be used for home loan applications?

Yes. Banks commonly request recent payslips to verify income, employment status and monthly salary consistency when reviewing home loan, car loan or personal loan applications.

Why is my net pay different every month?

Net pay may change because of overtime, bonus, unpaid leave, allowances, commission, PCB changes or other payroll adjustments.

Should bonus and overtime appear in a payslip?

Yes, if bonus or overtime is paid through payroll, it should normally appear as part of the payslip breakdown.

Can overtime increase PCB deductions?

Overtime may increase gross income for the month, which can affect payroll deductions and PCB depending on the employee's tax position and payroll calculation.

What should I do if my payslip is incorrect?

Check the details against your employment contract, attendance records and payroll records, then ask HR or payroll to clarify any incorrect amounts.

Is a payslip the same as an EA Form?

No. A payslip shows monthly salary details, while an EA Form summarises annual employment income and tax-related information for income tax filing.

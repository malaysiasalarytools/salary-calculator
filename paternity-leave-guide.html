What Is Paternity Leave?

Paternity leave is paid leave for eligible male employees when their wife gives birth. It allows fathers to support their spouse and newborn child during the early period after delivery.

This guide is written as a practical overview for employees, job seekers, HR staff and small business owners. Actual treatment may depend on employment contract terms, company policy and the latest legal requirements.

Paternity Leave Entitlement in Malaysia

Eligible fathers may be entitled to paternity leave when their spouse gives birth. In Malaysia, paternity leave is commonly referenced as 7 consecutive days of paid leave for eligible married male employees, subject to the latest employment requirements and company policy.

Paternity leave is meant to allow fathers to support their spouse and newborn child during the important period immediately after childbirth. Employees should check their employment contract, HR policy and the latest legal requirements to confirm how the entitlement applies in their workplace.

Note: This guide is a simplified employment guide for general understanding. For official, legal or dispute situations, employees and employers should check the latest law, employment contract, company policy and professional advice.

Who Is Eligible for Paternity Leave?

Eligibility for paternity leave may depend on employment status, marital status, length of service, supporting documents and other applicable requirements. Employees should not assume that every situation is treated the same way.

A father who wants to apply for paternity leave should review the company policy and inform HR early. If the company provides benefits that are better than the minimum requirement, the employment contract or employee handbook may explain the additional entitlement.

Notice Requirements for Paternity Leave

Employees are encouraged to notify their employer as early as possible before the expected delivery date. Early notice helps employers plan workloads, shift schedules, handover tasks and payroll processing during the leave period.

Employers may request supporting documents such as birth-related records, marriage documents or other documents required under company policy. Employees should keep written communication with HR so that the approved leave dates are clear.

Paternity Leave Pay

Eligible employees may continue receiving salary during approved paternity leave, subject to applicable employment requirements and company payroll procedures. The leave should normally be recorded properly in HR and payroll records.

Employees should review payslips and salary records after the leave period to ensure that paternity leave is reflected correctly and that salary was not incorrectly treated as unpaid leave.

EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB During Paternity Leave

Employees often ask whether statutory deductions continue during paternity leave. The actual treatment may depend on whether salary is paid during the leave period and how payroll processes the approved leave.

If salary is paid as usual, statutory deductions and contributions such as EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB may still appear in payroll records where applicable. Employees should review contribution records and payslips during and after paternity leave.

Payroll Item What Employees Should Check
EPF / KWSP Check whether employee and employer contributions are reflected correctly when salary is paid.
SOCSO / PERKESO Review payslip and contribution records if salary is processed during paternity leave.
EIS Check whether EIS is processed according to payroll treatment for the month.
PCB Monthly tax deduction may depend on taxable employment income and payroll calculation for the month.
Net Salary Take-home pay may differ if salary, deductions or allowances are treated differently during leave.

Paternity Leave vs Maternity Leave vs Annual Leave

Paternity leave is different from maternity leave and annual leave. Each leave type has a different purpose and should be recorded correctly in HR and payroll records.

Leave Type Purpose Who It Applies To
Paternity Leave Time off around childbirth to support spouse and newborn Eligible fathers
Maternity Leave Childbirth, recovery and childcare after delivery Eligible mothers
Annual Leave General planned paid time off Employees with annual leave entitlement

Returning to Work After Paternity Leave

Employees should communicate with HR and supervisors before returning to work to ensure leave records, schedules and payroll information remain accurate. This is especially useful if the leave period overlaps with public holidays, rest days or payroll cut-off dates.

A simple handover plan can help the employee return to normal duties smoothly. Employers should also ensure that the employee's approved paternity leave is recorded correctly and does not create unnecessary payroll disputes.

Common Paternity Leave Mistakes

Paternity leave is usually straightforward, but disputes may happen when notice, documents or payroll records are not handled properly. Employees and employers should keep clear written records.

  • Not informing the employer early before the expected delivery date
  • Missing supporting documents requested by HR
  • Not checking eligibility requirements before applying
  • Assuming paternity leave is automatically extended without approval
  • Not checking payslip and leave records after the leave period
  • Failing to communicate the exact leave dates clearly

Common Paternity Leave Examples

Example 1: Employee Gives Early Notice

The employee informs HR before the expected delivery date and prepares the required documents in advance. This helps the employer arrange work coverage and payroll processing before the leave starts.

Example 2: Payroll During Leave

The employee takes approved paternity leave and receives salary during the leave period. After payroll is processed, the employee checks payslip details and statutory deductions to ensure the leave was recorded correctly.

Example 3: Returning to Work

The employee confirms return-to-work arrangements with the supervisor and HR before resuming normal duties. This reduces confusion about leave balance, attendance records and work responsibilities.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many days of paternity leave are available in Malaysia?

Paternity leave is commonly referenced as 7 consecutive days of paid leave for eligible married male employees, subject to the latest employment requirements and company policy.

Who qualifies for paternity leave?

Eligibility may depend on employment status, marital status, length of service, supporting documents and other requirements. Employees should check their contract and company policy.

Is paternity leave paid?

Paternity leave is usually paid if the employee is eligible and the leave is approved according to applicable rules and company procedures.

Can paternity leave be extended?

Any extension beyond the approved paternity leave period may depend on company policy, annual leave, unpaid leave or other approved arrangements with the employer.

Do EPF, SOCSO and EIS continue during paternity leave?

If salary continues to be paid during approved paternity leave, statutory deductions and contributions such as EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB may still apply where relevant.

What documents are usually required for paternity leave?

Employers may request birth-related documents, marriage documents or other supporting records depending on company policy and HR procedures.

When should employees notify employers about paternity leave?

Employees should inform employers as early as possible before the expected delivery date so that HR can arrange leave records, workload planning and payroll processing.

Can employers offer more than the minimum paternity leave?

Yes. Some employers may provide paternity leave benefits that are better than the minimum requirement. Employees should check the employment contract or employee handbook.

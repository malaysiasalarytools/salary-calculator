Employee SOCSO vs Employer SOCSO

Employee SOCSO is deducted from the employee's salary. Employer SOCSO is paid by the employer on top of salary. Together, they support social security protection for eligible employees.

Note: Actual payroll results may differ because SOCSO is normally based on the official PERKESO contribution schedule, wage ceiling and category rules.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is SOCSO calculated in Malaysia?

SOCSO is normally calculated based on monthly wages and the applicable PERKESO contribution schedule. This calculator uses a simplified estimate for easier planning.

Is employer SOCSO deducted from my salary?

No. Employee SOCSO is deducted from your salary. Employer SOCSO is an additional contribution paid by the employer.

Why is my actual SOCSO different from this calculator?

Actual SOCSO may follow the official PERKESO wage table, contribution category and rounding rules, so it may differ from a simplified percentage estimate.

Does SOCSO affect take-home pay?

Yes. Employee SOCSO reduces monthly take-home pay slightly because it is deducted through payroll.

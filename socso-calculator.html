📌 SOCSO Calculator Quick Summary

This SOCSO calculator helps employees, job seekers, employers and payroll users estimate PERKESO contributions based on monthly salary and selected SOCSO category.

PERKESO SOCSO is part of Malaysia's social security protection system.
First Category Commonly covers Employment Injury and Invalidity schemes.
Second Category Commonly covers Employment Injury only.
Monthly SOCSO is usually calculated and paid through payroll.

Employee SOCSO

Employee SOCSO is deducted from monthly salary. It slightly reduces take-home pay and contributes to social security protection.

Employer SOCSO

Employer SOCSO is paid by the employer on top of salary. It is part of the employer's payroll cost.

SOCSO vs EPF: What Is the Difference?

EPF / KWSP

  • Retirement savings contribution.
  • Employee and employer contribute into EPF account.
  • Usually larger monthly deduction compared with SOCSO.

SOCSO / PERKESO

  • Social security protection contribution.
  • May cover employment injury and invalidity protection.
  • Usually smaller monthly deduction compared with EPF.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is SOCSO calculated in Malaysia?

SOCSO is normally calculated based on monthly wages, wage class and contribution category. This calculator uses a simplified estimate for easier planning.

Is employer SOCSO deducted from my salary?

No. Employee SOCSO is deducted from your salary. Employer SOCSO is an additional contribution paid by your employer on top of your salary.

What is First Category SOCSO?

First Category is commonly used for Employment Injury and Invalidity protection. In this calculator, it estimates both employee and employer portions.

What is Second Category SOCSO?

Second Category is commonly used for Employment Injury protection only. In this calculator, it estimates employer contribution only.

Why is my actual SOCSO different from this calculator?

Actual SOCSO may follow the official PERKESO wage table, wage ceiling, category rules and rounding rules, so it may differ from a simplified percentage estimate.

Does SOCSO affect take-home pay?

Yes. Employee SOCSO reduces monthly take-home pay slightly because it is deducted through payroll.

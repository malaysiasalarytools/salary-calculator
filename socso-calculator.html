How SOCSO Is Calculated in Malaysia

SOCSO, also known as PERKESO, is a social security contribution for eligible employees in Malaysia. It is normally calculated based on monthly wages, wage class and SOCSO category. Employee SOCSO is deducted from salary, while employer SOCSO is paid by the employer on top of salary.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is SOCSO calculated in Malaysia?

SOCSO is normally calculated based on monthly wages, wage class and contribution category. This calculator uses a simplified estimate for easier planning.

Is employer SOCSO deducted from my salary?

No. Employee SOCSO is deducted from your salary. Employer SOCSO is an additional contribution paid by your employer on top of your salary.

What is First Category SOCSO?

First Category is commonly used for Employment Injury and Invalidity protection. In this calculator, it estimates both employee and employer portions.

What is Second Category SOCSO?

Second Category is commonly used for Employment Injury protection only. In this calculator, it estimates employer contribution only.

Why is my actual SOCSO different from this calculator?

Actual SOCSO may follow the official PERKESO wage table, wage ceiling, category rules and rounding rules, so it may differ from a simplified percentage estimate.

Does SOCSO affect take-home pay?

Yes. Employee SOCSO reduces monthly take-home pay slightly because it is deducted through payroll.

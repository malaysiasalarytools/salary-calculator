What is SOCSO in Malaysia?

SOCSO, also known as PERKESO (Pertubuhan Keselamatan Sosial), is Malaysia's social security organisation that provides protection and financial assistance to employees who suffer from workplace accidents, occupational diseases, disability or other employment-related risks.

SOCSO was established to ensure that employees and their dependants receive financial support and medical assistance when unexpected employment-related incidents occur.

Most employers in Malaysia are required to register eligible employees under SOCSO and make monthly contributions according to the contribution rates prescribed by PERKESO.

Who Needs To Contribute To SOCSO?

SOCSO contributions are generally mandatory for Malaysian employees and permanent residents working under a contract of service.

Both employers and employees contribute to SOCSO. The contribution amount depends on the employee's monthly salary and follows the official contribution schedule issued by PERKESO.

Employers are responsible for deducting the employee portion and remitting both employer and employee contributions to PERKESO every month.

What Benefits Does SOCSO Provide?

SOCSO provides several forms of protection and financial assistance for employees.

  • Medical Benefits
  • Temporary Disablement Benefits
  • Permanent Disablement Benefits
  • Dependants' Benefits
  • Funeral Benefits
  • Rehabilitation Benefits
  • Education Benefits

These benefits help employees and their families cope with financial difficulties arising from workplace accidents, disabilities or death.

How Is SOCSO Calculated?

SOCSO contributions are calculated based on the employee's monthly wages and the official contribution table published by PERKESO.

The contribution amount is shared between employers and employees. Higher salaries generally result in higher contribution amounts, subject to contribution limits established by PERKESO.

Why Is SOCSO Important?

Many employees focus only on their monthly salary and EPF savings, but SOCSO provides an additional layer of protection that can become extremely valuable when accidents or disabilities occur.

Without SOCSO protection, employees may need to bear significant medical costs and income losses themselves.

SOCSO helps reduce financial hardship by providing benefits and compensation to eligible workers and their dependants.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SOCSO mandatory in Malaysia?

Yes. SOCSO is generally mandatory for eligible employees and employers in Malaysia.

Who pays SOCSO contributions?

Both employees and employers contribute to SOCSO according to the applicable contribution rates.

What is PERKESO?

PERKESO is the Malaysian Social Security Organisation responsible for administering SOCSO benefits and contributions.

Can foreign workers contribute to SOCSO?

Certain categories of foreign workers may also be covered under SOCSO regulations. Employers should refer to current PERKESO guidelines.

Does SOCSO affect take-home salary?

Yes. The employee portion of SOCSO contributions is deducted from monthly salary and therefore reduces take-home pay.

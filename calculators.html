📊 How Salary Is Calculated in Malaysia

Calculating your take-home pay means subtracting statutory deductions from your gross monthly salary. In Malaysia, the main deductions are usually EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB.

Net Salary = Gross Salary − Total Deductions Total Deductions = EPF + SOCSO + EIS + PCB

🛡️ Tax Relief & Payroll Notes

Click each tax relief below to see common claim limits and examples.

Lifestyle Relief

Claim up to RM2,500 for books, smartphone, laptop, internet subscription and gym membership.

Medical Expenses

Claim up to RM8,000 for parents' medical treatment, serious diseases and related healthcare expenses.

Education Fees

Claim tax relief for approved self-education fees and professional courses.

Insurance & Takaful

Life insurance and takaful premiums may qualify for tax relief up to applicable limits.

EPF Contributions

EPF contributions can be claimed up to RM4,000 (combined with certain insurance reliefs).

Child Relief

RM2,000 per child under 18. RM8,000 per child in tertiary education.

Payroll Note: EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB deductions are generally processed by employers through monthly payroll submissions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this salary calculator include EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB?

Yes. This calculator estimates your monthly take-home pay after deducting EPF, SOCSO, EIS and estimated PCB tax based on your gross monthly salary.

Why is my actual salary different from the calculator result?

Actual payroll calculations may vary depending on tax relief claims, additional allowances, bonuses, overtime payments, unpaid leave and employer payroll settings.

How is PCB tax calculated in Malaysia?

PCB (Potongan Cukai Bulanan) is a monthly tax deduction system administered by LHDN. The amount depends on your chargeable income, tax reliefs and personal circumstances.

Can I calculate annual salary from monthly salary?

Yes. This calculator automatically provides both monthly and annual salary breakdowns based on the gross monthly salary entered.

Do bonuses and allowances affect take-home pay?

Yes. Additional income such as bonuses, commissions, allowances and overtime payments may increase statutory deductions and PCB tax.

What tax reliefs can reduce PCB deductions?

Common Malaysia tax reliefs include EPF contributions, life insurance, medical insurance, lifestyle expenses, education fees, medical expenses and child reliefs.

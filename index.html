What is EPF?

What is EPF in Malaysia?

The Employees Provident Fund (EPF), also known as KWSP, is Malaysia's mandatory retirement savings scheme for employees. Both employees and employers contribute a percentage of monthly salary into the EPF account.

EPF contributions help employees build long-term retirement savings. The contribution rate varies depending on age and government policy. For most employees below 60 years old, employee contribution is generally 11% of monthly salary.

Funds accumulated in EPF can be withdrawn under specific conditions, including retirement, housing purchases, education and approved medical expenses.

What is SOCSO?

SOCSO provides protection for workplace injury and disability.

What is EIS?

EIS gives financial support if you lose your job.

What is PCB?

PCB is monthly income tax deduction based on Malaysian tax system.

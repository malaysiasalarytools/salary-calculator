How Salary Is Calculated in Malaysia

Gross salary is reduced by EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB tax in Malaysia.

What is EPF in Malaysia?

The Employees Provident Fund (EPF), also known as KWSP, is Malaysia's mandatory retirement savings scheme for employees. Both employees and employers contribute a percentage of monthly salary into the EPF account.

EPF contributions help employees build long-term retirement savings. The contribution rate varies depending on age and government policy. For most employees below 60 years old, employee contribution is generally 11% of monthly salary.

Funds accumulated in EPF can be withdrawn under specific conditions, including retirement, housing purchases, education and approved medical expenses.

Malaysia EPF Contribution Rates 2026

For most Malaysian employees below the age of 60, the employee EPF contribution rate is generally 11% of monthly wages. Employers are also required to contribute to EPF on behalf of employees, subject to prevailing EPF contribution schedules.

EPF contribution rates may be revised by the government from time to time. Employees should refer to official EPF guidelines for the latest contribution rates.

What is SOCSO?

SOCSO provides protection for workplace injury and disability.

Malaysia SOCSO Contribution Rates 2026

SOCSO contributions are shared between employers and employees. The contribution amount depends on the employee's monthly wages and follows the contribution schedule issued by PERKESO.

SOCSO provides protection against workplace injuries, occupational diseases, invalidity and other employment-related risks. Both employers and employees are generally required to contribute.

What is EIS?

EIS gives financial support if you lose your job.

What is PCB?

PCB is monthly income tax deduction based on Malaysian tax system.

Example Salary Calculation (RM5,000 Monthly Salary)

For an employee earning RM5,000 per month, deductions may include EPF, SOCSO, EIS and PCB tax. After these deductions, the take-home salary will be lower than the gross salary.

For example, a RM5,000 monthly salary may result in deductions for retirement savings, social security protection and income tax. The final net salary depends on the applicable contribution and tax rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much EPF do employees contribute?

Most Malaysian employees contribute 11% of their monthly salary to EPF, subject to current EPF regulations.

Is SOCSO mandatory in Malaysia?

SOCSO contributions are generally mandatory for eligible employees and employers in Malaysia.

What is PCB tax?

PCB (Potongan Cukai Bulanan) is a monthly tax deduction system used to collect income tax throughout the year.

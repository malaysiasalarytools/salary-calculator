Malaysia Salary Breakdown Explained

This salary calculator helps you estimate your take-home pay in Malaysia after mandatory deductions such as EPF, SOCSO, EIS, and income tax.

What is EPF?

EPF (Employees Provident Fund) is a mandatory retirement savings scheme in Malaysia. Employees contribute around 11% of monthly salary while employers also contribute additional percentage.

What is SOCSO?

SOCSO provides social security protection for employees in Malaysia, covering workplace injuries and disability protection.

What is EIS?

EIS (Employment Insurance System) provides financial assistance and job support if you lose your job.

Income Tax (PCB)

PCB is monthly tax deduction based on your annual income. Higher income results in higher tax rate under Malaysia's progressive tax system.

FAQ

Why is my net salary lower than my offer letter?
Because offer letter shows gross salary before deductions.

Are EPF and SOCSO mandatory?
Yes, for most employees in Malaysia.
